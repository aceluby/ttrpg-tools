# 32 - Queen In Shadow

- Game: Corona Eclipsa
- System: Pathfinder 2e
- Duration: 2 hours
- Tone: desperate intrigue, stealth, hunted urgency

## Clarifying Answers Locked In

- Session length: 2 hours
- Opening mode: intrigue and stealth in `Willow Woods`
- Succession state: the realm is split between `Rhaegar` and `Dorian` loyalties
- `Serel` is watched by `Rhaegar`'s people, but the exact hands are unclear
- `Nyx` is only now learning the party survived
- The king died of natural decline after a visible illness
- `Dorian`'s destination remains vague, sharpened by dreams and signs rather than a full map
- Marcel's prophecy should echo directly multiple times this session
- Rival pursuers include `Rhaegar`'s forces, hired bandits, and a nearby `Corterie`
- Pursuer evidence should appear midway through the journey
- `Serel`'s extraction should be a real stealth sequence
- `Renna` stays behind to study `Dax`'s amulet
- `Dax` gets a trust-pressure beat around leaving the amulet behind
- One major encounter max
- `Dorian` is found pinned down
- The attacking force is backed by `Rhaenard`
- End state: camp after the rescue approach, with `Dorian` weave-dazed and Marcel urged toward a vision
- Emotional target: desperation

## Assumptions

- This session begins the moment `Dax` meets `Nyx`'s eyes in `Willow Woods`.
- The group has just completed the harsh return from the pocket-dimension escape and arrives worn down but functional.
- `Willow Woods` is not fully hostile territory, but it is no longer safe for obvious allies of `Dorian` and `Serel`.
- The session should spend about half its time on extraction and briefing, then pivot into a fast northbound push.
- The final confrontation is not the battle itself; it is the discovery that `Dorian` is alive, besieged, and only one
  hard move away from death.

## Characters

- `Harlan`
    - Spotlight: silent movement, scouting watchers, and reading the ground once the pursuit starts
    - Useful pressure: he should feel how little margin the group has if stealth breaks early, especially once
      `Nighteyes` refuses the walls
- `Dax`
    - Spotlight: deciding whether to leave the amulet with `Renna` and trusting someone else with dragon-linked power
    - Useful pressure: every instinct says keep it close, but speed and secrecy argue otherwise
- `Jin`
    - Spotlight: keeping the group morally and practically aligned under stress
    - Useful pressure: he has to help move `Serel` through fear without slowing everyone else down
- `Marcel`
    - Spotlight: disguises, social misdirection, and prophetic recognition
    - Useful pressure: the dream keeps becoming real in pieces and everyone needs him steady anyway
- `Serel`
    - Spotlight: she is frightened but not passive, and should move like someone already deciding what sacrifice costs
    - Useful pressure: she wants `Dorian`, not safety
- `Nyx`
    - Spotlight: fast briefing, route planning, and practical distrust
    - Useful pressure: she has to act before the shock of seeing them alive turns into lost time

## Strong Start

- Recap:
    - Last we left off, Corona Eclipsa.... spent five hard days clawing their way back through heat, hunger, and
      uncertainty. `Harlan` and `Nighteyes` kept the group fed, `Dax`, `Jin`, and `Renna` found the water that kept
      everyone moving, and every choice to survive cost precious time.
    - At last you reached `Willow Woods`, the walled lakeside town of gardens and noble summer homes, only to find the
      guards tense and every conversation half-whispered. The king is dead. `Prince Dorian` is missing. Rumor says
      `Rhaenard` has named himself king. And just as the truth began to settle in, `Dax` met the eyes of `Nyx`, hood up,
      already heading straight toward you.
- Opening image:
    - Willow Woods glows with wealth and spring beauty, but the beauty suddenly feels trapped. Painted gates stand shut,
      guards scan every road, and the lake beyond the garden walls looks too calm for the fear hanging over the town.
      - Nighteyes at the wall.
        - Before the group leaves, give `Harlan` a short quiet scene with `Nighteyes`.
        - `Nighteyes` does not want the walls, the stone, or the smell of trapped people.
        - He makes clear in his own way that he will not go inside and will wait beyond the walls or find `Harlan` after.
        - This should feel like trust, not abandonment: `Nighteyes` is choosing his own line and still choosing `Harlan`.
        - Let `Harlan` decide whether to push, accept, or promise to return quickly.
- Immediate problem:
    - `Nyx` does not slow down. She says only, "Follow," and turns toward a narrow lane between summer kitchens. If the
      party follows, she leads them through a few alleys and into a large yard overlooking `Willow Lake`. There sits a
      locked cellar. `Nyx` unlocks it and steps down into an unassuming room lined with shelves of mostly spoiled food.
      At the back she twists a jar full of cloudy liquid, opening a hidden door with stairs descending into an unlit
      tunnel. She lights a torch and says, "Lock the cellar and follow me. The door is on a timer and will close in a
      minute."
    - The tunnels are dark and cramped. As she leads the group through twists and turns, `Nyx` says, "Everyone thought
      you were dead." At the far end she presses a small rock and a wall panel slides open, revealing a brightly lit
      room overlooking the lake and `Princess Serel` staring out the window.

## Scenes

- Nyx, Serel, and the party.
    - Let the first real scene be a tense conversation, not a monologue.
    - `Nyx` is practical and urgent:
        - everyone thought the party died
        - the king declined fast and finally passed
        - `Rhaegar` moved immediately
        - staying still is no longer safe
    - `Serel` carries the emotional and political core:
        - she is being politely imprisoned
        - the realm is splitting by whispers, favors, and fear
        - `Dorian` left because the dreams would not stop
        - she will not let `Rhaegar` find him first
    - The party gets room to ask questions, challenge assumptions, and react to being presumed dead.
    - Marcel should hear direct echoes of his earlier prophecy in the details `Serel` gives.
- The amulet decision.
    - `Renna` asks to stay behind and study `Dax`'s amulet, believing she may unlock more of its power or meaning.
    - This should not be a bookkeeping choice; it is about trust.
    - If `Dax` leaves it, reward that decision later with a stronger omen or future breakthrough.
    - If he refuses, preserve that tension and let the amulet remain a physical risk on the road.
- Sneak `Serel` out of Willow Woods.
    - Marcel's ritual disguise should matter here and feel clever, not automatic.
    - Build a real stealth sequence through servants' lanes, garden walls, closed kitchens, and watchful patrols.
    - Good obstacles:
        - a checkpoint where a guard knows one face too well
        - a house servant loyal to `Dorian` who helps if reassured
        - a barking kennel or startled horse that threatens to expose them
        - one watcher who is not a soldier and may be reporting to someone worse
    - `Serel` should contribute with speed, memory of the grounds, and ruthless clarity once the choice is made.
- The queen in hiding.
    - Once outside, `Serel` fills in the emotional truth:
        - everyone assumed the party died
        - `Dorian` would not sit still once the king was gone
        - he kept dreaming of northern mountains, a carved dragon, and something waiting above the snow line
        - she refuses to let `Rhaegar` define the story by finding `Dorian` first
    - This is where you can directly echo Marcel's dream imagery.
- Midway signs of pursuit.
    - Use physical evidence first:
        - split horse tracks
        - expensive arrows in cheap bandit bodies
        - a campfire circle with mage ash or ritual residue
        - a dropped `Corterie` clue Marcel recognizes
    - The point is not "combat now," but "run harder, someone is close."
- Final set piece: Dorian pinned down.
    - The party sees `Dorian` below or ahead, trapped by `Rhaenard`-backed bandits using elevation, arrow slits, or
      broken ruins as a killing bowl.
    - Run this as a pressure map rather than normal trading of blows:
        - repeated group Reflex saves to dodge arrow storms
        - movement between sparse cover
        - one or two critical path choices to reach `Dorian`
    - End before full resolution if needed:
        - the party reaches hard cover near him
        - `Dorian` is alive but weave-dazed
        - camp follows after a brutal retreat or temporary disengage
- Mountain ascent and the stone field.
    - The next day or shortly after, the party reaches the specific mountain site `Dorian` has been chasing.
    - There are several massive stones, old carved statues of kings, knights, and dragons, and a clear sense that this
      place was built to receive power.
    - `Dorian` begins shaping one of the largest stones with the weave.
    - Some of the excess weave is drawn into the older carvings nearby.
    - Beside `Dorian`'s working stone is a statue of a girl riding a dragon.
    - With a high enough Perception check, the party can see that the excess weave is being pulled specifically into
      that rider statue.
    - On an especially strong result, they may see it shift or breathe.
- The northern pass approach.
  - The air grows colder, the path narrows, and the dream echoes sharpen.
  - Good direct echoes for Marcel:
      - a girl carved into a weathered dragon statue
      - a ridgeline that matches the shape from the dream
      - signs of recent violence below a high stone ledge
- Days at the mountain.
    - The party may spend 2-3 days here if pacing allows.
    - Use this time for grief, worry, and the sense that they are helping build something they do not fully understand.
    - `Marcel` can use his own actions and weave connection to feed power into the rider statue.
    - No one else can do this in the same way.
    - `Dorian` is partly shaping the stones on purpose and partly being used as a conduit by forces larger than his own
      will.
- First overnight scene: Serel reaches through Marcel.
    - After the party reaches relative safety, `Dorian` slips in and out of himself.
    - His speech, gestures, and weave use often feel automatic, as though part of him is elsewhere and the body is
      trying to keep up.
    - `Serel`, frightened and exhausted, asks `Marcel` if he can touch the weave and try to reach him.
    - If `Marcel` does, he finds a partly transformed `Dorian` in a deeply uncomfortable weave-space full of pressure,
      prophecy, and cold certainty.
    - `Dorian` explains:
        - the weave has promised him a weapon
        - he must release the dragons
        - he can only do that by going deeper into the weave
    - The scene should sound half visionary and half alarming, as though `Dorian` no longer knows where conviction ends
      and influence begins.
- Second overnight scene: the switch.
    - In the middle of the night, `Serel` asks `Marcel` again to try to contact `Dorian`.
    - This time `Dorian` is more frightened, more distant, and less certain he will survive what is happening to him.
    - He asks `Marcel` to temporarily switch places with him.
    - If `Marcel` agrees, he experiences only enough of `Dorian`'s state to understand how painful, prophetic, and
      invasive the weave has become.
    - Back in the waking world, `Serel` ends up in bed with `Marcel`'s body but `Dorian`'s mind inside it.
    - Keep the scene unsettling, grief-struck, and quietly ambiguous rather than explicit.
    - This should remain secret between `Marcel`, `Serel`, and `Dorian` unless someone chooses otherwise.
- Dax and Jin mountain beats.
    - `Dax` should feel that the mountain site is not dead stone. Something in it recognizes dragon-blood and expects
      witness, service, or judgment.
    - Good options:
        - the rider statue seems to turn toward him when no one else is looking
        - the carved dragons feel less like art and more like sleeping shapes
        - the choice to leave or reclaim the amulet starts to feel cosmically important
    - `Jin` should get a beat of spiritual wrongness:
        - this is not simple destiny, it is surrender to something vast
        - `Dorian` may be trying to save people through a path that is consuming him
        - the weave here feels less like blessing and more like a force demanding a vessel
- Corterie interruption.
    - If the session or a future follow-up reaches this point, the calm at the mountain is broken by the arrival of the
      `Corterie`.
    - They come as trackers and enemies, not negotiators.
    - Their arrival should interrupt either:
        - a grief-heavy conversation between `Marcel` and `Serel`
        - `Dorian`'s shaping of the stones
        - the first clear sign that the rider statue can awaken
    - This interruption should make it obvious that the party is running out of time and that whatever weapon `Dorian`
      is becoming may not belong to them once awakened.

## Secrets and Clues

- The king truly died of natural decline, which makes the succession crisis even uglier because no clean villain caused
  it.
- `Rhaegar` moved immediately to consolidate power the moment the king died.
- `Willow Woods` is functionally a gilded holding pen for politically inconvenient nobles.
- `Serel` is treated with surface courtesy because openly harming her would damage `Rhaegar`'s legitimacy.
- `Dorian` left willingly, but not calmly; the visions had become too specific to ignore.
- Marcel's prophecy is not symbolic noise. It is tracking real events.
- `Rhaegar`'s men are not the only hunters on the road; hired blades and the `Corterie` are following the same scent.
- The `Corterie` is close enough that the party should assume magical interference or theft pressure soon.
- `Dorian` reached the right mountains, but not before his enemies did.
- Whatever `Dorian` is seeking is tied to carved dragons, old stone, and mountain-top weave power.
- `Dorian` is not merely following visions anymore; he is partly submerged in them.
- The promised "weapon" is tied to stone dragons rather than a forged blade or spell.
- The rider statue can receive excess weave, but `Marcel` is the one most capable of awakening it.
- If `Dorian` succeeds fully, he may stop being only himself.
- `Serel` is desperate enough to sanction things she does not understand.
- The mountain site was built to shape rulers, riders, and dragons together.

## Fantastic Locations

- Willow Woods
    - Rendering: white garden walls, iron gates, flowering paths, and a lakeside town trying to look peaceful while
      everyone watches everyone else.
    - Deeper details: expensive homes sit too quiet, conversations stop when strangers pass, and even the guards seem
      unsure whose orders will matter tomorrow.
- Service Lanes Behind The Summer Houses
    - Rendering: laundry lines, herb gardens, hot kitchen walls, and narrow stone passages made for workers, not nobles.
    - Deeper details: perfect stealth terrain if the party moves fast, but every corner risks a witness instead of a
      warrior.
- Northern Road Cut
    - Rendering: old wagon ruts, thinning trees, mountain wind, and signs that many riders passed this way too recently.
    - Deeper details: broken fletching, camp ash, and boot prints tell a story of multiple pursuing groups moving with
      different discipline.
- Dragon Stair Ruins
    - Rendering: weathered steps cut into the mountain, shattered dragon statuary, and a kill-box of stone shelves and
      arrow angles.
    - Deeper details: the place feels half shrine and half battlefield, with weave residue clinging to broken carvings
      and recent blood on old rock.
- The Stone Field
    - Rendering: enormous worked stones, half-finished carvings, old kings and dragons watching from the snow line, and
      `Dorian` bleeding power into the mountain.
    - Deeper details: some statues are already carved, some are waiting, and the rider-girl statue seems to drink in
      stray weave like a thirst it has carried for centuries.
- Night Camp Beneath The Carvings
    - Rendering: thin firelight, cold wind, sleeping bedrolls, and one man who never seems fully asleep because part of
      him is elsewhere.
    - Deeper details: the mountain hums after dark, dreams feel close to waking, and every quiet conversation risks
      becoming a confession.

## Important NPCs

- Nyx
    - Role: covert ally and extraction coordinator
    - Motivation: get `Serel` out, get to `Dorian` first, and stay ahead of `Rhaegar`'s response
    - Trait: eyes move before the rest of her does and she speaks like every second costs blood
- `Princess Serel Aiuvarin`
    - Role: politically trapped royal ally, now the nearest thing to a queen in exile
    - Motivation: find `Dorian` before `Rhaegar` does and keep the alliance from dying with the king
    - Trait: speaks quickly once she has decided and keeps her fear folded into action
- Renna
    - Role: temporary rear support and arcane researcher
    - Motivation: unlock the truth or power in `Dax`'s amulet before the wrong people do
    - Trait: goes still when a magical idea hooks her, as if the rest of the room stopped existing
- `Prince Dorian Vaelor`
    - Role: missing heir and trusted ally
    - Motivation: follow the visions to whatever power or truth the mountains hold before the realm collapses around him
    - Trait: gives a slight upward nod before speaking, even when half-broken
    - Current state:
        - partly transformed by repeated contact with the weave
        - sometimes moves and speaks as if on delayed instructions from somewhere deeper
- Hired Reaver Captain
    - Role: field commander for the final pressure scene
    - Motivation: pin `Dorian`, claim the reward, and leave before mountain magic or royal politics get worse
    - Trait: speaks in practical battlefield barks and wipes arrow grease across one thumb before every volley
- Rider Statue
    - Role: dormant dragon-rider figure that can receive and eventually answer weave power
    - Motivation: unknown, but receptive to `Marcel` in a way the other statues are not
    - Trait: still face, lifted chin, and the unsettling impression of someone waiting to be remembered

## Monsters or Threats

- Willow Woods Watch Net
    - Threat type: social surveillance and stealth pressure
    - Use:
        - 3 failed group stealth or deception beats means the town quietly starts closing around them
        - on the first failure, a watcher tails them
        - on the second, a checkpoint stiffens or a route closes
        - on the third, they must break contact fast or fight their way out
- Group stealth procedure
    - Best skills:
        - Stealth, Deception, Society, Thievery, or a smart spell use
    - Suggested structure:
        - 4 successes before 3 failures to get `Serel` out cleanly
        - `DC 21` baseline because the town is tense and watchers are primed
        - reduce to `DC 19` where Marcel's disguises clearly carry the moment
        - increase to `DC 23` at checkpoints or if anyone acts like they belong too much
- Road pursuit pressure
    - Threat type: being hunted rather than full combat
    - Use:
        - one failed travel beat means the pursuers gain ground
        - two failed beats means the party starts seeing fresh signs of the same hunters every few hours
        - three failed beats means the final encounter begins with worse positioning
- Final arrow-kill-box encounter
    - Threat type: set piece rescue under fire
    - Structure:
        - each round of exposed movement, the whole party makes `DC 22` Reflex saves against coordinated volleys
        - Success: no damage and keep moving
        - Failure: 2d6 piercing damage and lose ground
        - Critical failure: 4d6 piercing damage and fall prone or slide into worse cover
    - Enemy package:
        - 6 level 4 bandit archers in layered cover
        - 2 level 5 shield raiders guarding the approach lanes
        - 1 level 6 captain directing volleys
  - Table note:
    - this should feel like a dangerous obstacle course with enemies, not a static fair fight
    - reward route-finding, cover destruction, smoke, illusion, and bold rushes
- Weave contact with `Dorian`
  - Threat type: prophetic contact and identity erosion
  - Use:
    - when `Marcel` reaches through the weave, the cost is discomfort, distorted memory, and unwanted revelation
    - this should never feel like safe dream-sending
    - a failed or badly handled reach can leave `Marcel` drained, shaken, or carrying fragments of `Dorian`'s fear
- Awakening the rider statue
  - Threat type: tempting power
  - Use:
    - only `Marcel` can meaningfully feed his own weave into the rider statue
    - each contribution should feel powerful, risky, and narratively significant
    - if you want mechanics, require repeated successful weave-facing checks and track visible changes in the statue

## Treasure / Rewards

- Escape with `Serel`, which is itself a major narrative win
- Confirmation that `Dorian` is alive
- A clearer line from Marcel's dream to the northern dragon mystery
- If `Dax` leaves the amulet with `Renna`, promise a future breakthrough, stronger mount use, or new unlocked function
- Documents, insignia, or field orders from the bandits linking the pursuit back to `Rhaenard`
- Access to the stone field and the first real path toward the dragon weapon

## GM Notes

- This is a short session. Spend your time on:
    - `Nyx`
    - the stealth extraction
    - `Serel`'s briefing
    - pursuit signs
    - the final desperate reveal of `Dorian`
- Let Willow Woods feel beautiful enough that the fear underneath it becomes more unsettling.
- `Serel` should not read as helpless. She is under pressure, but once moving she is decisive.
- Keep the `Corterie` nearby but mostly offscreen. They are best used here as a pressure multiplier, not the main
  problem.
- If pacing runs long, end the session the moment the party reaches cover near `Dorian`.
- If pacing runs fast, let camp happen after a temporary disengage and move into the first overnight weave contact.
- Treat everything after the initial `Dorian` reveal as loaded future content that can start now or carry cleanly into
  the next session.
- Vision beat for Marcel:
    - he sees `Dorian` on a mountain peak carving a dragon from living stone
    - the carving becomes a stone dragon filled with weave light
    - the dragon rises and annihilates their enemies in a monstrous battle of ice, stone, and blue-white fire
    - the vision should feel triumphant for one breath and catastrophic the next
- Grief beat for `Serel`:
    - once `Dorian` is physically present again, even in a broken state, let her finally lose some of the rigid control
      she used to survive `Willow Woods`
- Keep the body-switch scene ambiguous.
    - The point is not to explain exactly what happened.
    - The point is to leave `Marcel`, `Serel`, and the table unsettled by how far gone `Dorian` already is.
