# 30 - Pocket Dimension Escape

- Game: Corona Eclipsa
- System: Pathfinder 2e
- Duration: 2 hours
- Tone: grimdark fantasy, dangerous intrigue

## Assumptions

- The party still does not know the trapped wizard's truth, the artifact's real value, or the full scope of the
  `Corterie`'s goals.
- The session should prioritize playable content and pressure over a fixed ending.
- If the party does not escape this session, the prep should still generate a strong midpoint of betrayal, revelation,
  and directional momentum.

## Characters

- `Harlan`
    - Spotlight: deepen the bond with `Nighteyes`
    - Useful pressure: reading danger before others do, sensing deception, choosing trust or distance
- `Jin`
    - Spotlight: perception or spiritual insight
    - Useful pressure: noticing hidden wrongness, reading intent, sensing what should not exist in this place
- `Dax`
    - Spotlight: reaction to the dragon god and dragon-linked space beyond reality
    - Useful pressure: primal recognition, destiny tug, impatience with puzzles or half-truths
- `Marcel`
    - Useful pressure: the `Corterie` knows how to needle him, exclude him, and talk around him

## Strong Start

- Recap:
    - Last we left off, Corona Eclipsa... the party entered the pocket dimension in pursuit of answers and a possible
      weapon against the `hollowed`, knowing that `the Corterie` was already days ahead of them. Instead of arriving in
      secret or safety, they were thrown straight into a pitched battle inside a place that clearly does not obey
      ordinary rules of space, time, or intent.
    - In the aftermath of that chaos, the party and `the Corterie` have been forced into a tense temporary alignment
      neither side truly wants. Most recently, they survived a brush with one of the dimension's anti-magic golems by
      choosing restraint over force, learning that this place reacts to violence and magic as if it were built to punish
      exactly those instincts.
- Opening image:
    - Marble floor and walls veined like old bone, fresh signs of the recent battle still scattered across the chamber,
      huge pillars casting long shadows, the `Corterie` bruised and frayed but still dangerous, and one silent door
      ahead.
    - You see the three group together, drink some potions, with a few quiet whispers that are just barely audible.
    - From here out we will begin doing actions in 10 minute stretches, roll for dungeon initiative
- Immediate tension:
    - Everyone now knows this place reacts to violence and magic.
    - The obvious next move requires temporary cooperation with people the party should not trust.

## Scenes

- Uneasy truce in the marble hall.
    - Let `Tiny` push for practical cooperation while hiding urgency.
    - Let `George` look ready to break the truce if anyone reaches for power first.
    - `Elys Merdye` watches more than she speaks.
- Enter the pit chamber.
    - A vast 100 foot hole splits the room with an island and central door in the middle.
    - Two side options are clear: the office route and the puzzle route.
    - The chamber should feel designed to force a choice under pressure.
- Puzzle-room route.
    - Solving the puzzle opens the bridge to the island door.
    - Build the puzzle so it rewards observation and restraint over brute magical forcing.
    - `Jin` can notice spiritual or symbolic logic in the design.
- Office route.
    - The office contains the caged demon slave, the artifact, and several locked doors.
    - This room should tempt shortcuts, bargains, or bad decisions.
    - The demon knows enough to mislead cleverly while still revealing useful truth.
- Harlan and `Nighteyes` beat.
    - Use a moment of instinct, warning, or emotional recognition where `Nighteyes` treats Harlan as `changer` or
      `brother`.
    - This should matter in the fiction, not just as flavor.
- Dragon-god threshold.
    - Beyond the island door is vast nothing controlled by thought.
    - The dragon god's presence should register immediately to `Dax` as something ancient, real, and overwhelming.
    - This can be awe, fear, calling, or recognition.
    - Give `Dax` at least one moment that feels personal, as though the presence recognizes something in his blood
      before it recognizes the rest of the party.
- `Corterie` betrayal.
    - Once the route out or the real truth becomes clear, the `Corterie` makes its move.
    - The betrayal should come from self-interest and prior orders, not random cruelty.
    - It should confirm they are dangerous and not merely school rivals.
- Escape or hard directional turn.
    - Best outcome: the party gets out.
    - If not, end with the path out clearly in hand and the `Corterie` exposed.

## Secrets and Clues

- `The Corterie` works for `Rhaegar`.
- A great deal of outside time has already passed while the party has been inside.
- The artifact is mostly useless as a true solution.
- The trapped wizard is not a prize to free casually.
- This place is built to punish uncontrolled magic use.
- The `Corterie` knew more than they admitted before entering.
- The dragon god exists outside ordinary reality and is not just another dungeon guardian.
- The fastest way out may not be the safest way out.
- The demon slave knows enough to point at the trap without understanding the whole cosmic picture.
- The party is being pushed toward a choice, not simply toward a locked exit.

## Fantastic Locations

- Marble Hall
    - Rendering: polished marble, towering pillars, battered rivals, one waiting door, and the hush that comes after a
      thing chooses not to kill you.
    - Deeper details: faint scrape marks from old constructs, hairline sigils in the stone, no dust, no natural echo.
- Pit Chamber
    - Rendering: a 100 foot void, a lonely stone island, a central door, and side passages that feel like arguments
      made architecture.
    - Deeper details: the drop swallows sound oddly, the island seems slightly closer when stared at, and the room
      punishes haste.
- Office Of Chains
    - Rendering: shelves, locks, records, and a caged demon slave beside the artifact everyone came to find.
    - Deeper details: several locked doors hint at older containment, paper fragments imply deliberate information
      removal, and the room feels more bureaucratic than mystical.
- Thought-Void Threshold
    - Rendering: the door opens onto vast nothing, where distance means little and the world bends around will.
    - Deeper details: thought has weight here, fear can shape perception, and something immense watches from beyond
      reality.

## Important NPCs

### `Tiny`

- Role: mouth and brains of the `Corterie`
- Motivation: get what the `Corterie` came for while using the party as tools if necessary
- Trait: talks like cooperation is the only adult option

### `George`

- Role: breach specialist of the `Corterie`
- Motivation: back the `Corterie`'s play and intimidate anyone slowing them down
- Trait: smiles only when the plan gets uglier

### `Elys Merdye`

- Role: silent recovery specialist of the `Corterie`
- Motivation: secure the target, exploit confusion, and leave before anyone understands what was taken
- Trait: studies everyone before committing to action

### Caged Demon Slave

- Role: imprisoned source of partial truth, temptation, and bad bargains
- Motivation: gain leverage, freedom, or revenge through whoever seems easiest to sway
- Trait: answers too fast when a truth will still hurt you
- Description: gaunt, long-limbed, and half-starved, with cracked crimson skin, iron collar scars, yellow eyes that
  never blink enough, and a grin that keeps breaking into private laughter
- Voice notes:
    - he speaks like someone who has gone a little hollow from solitude
    - he hints at terrible things casually, then recoils when he gets too close to naming his master
    - he should sound dangerous in his own right, but more frightened of disobedience than of death

#### Cryptic Demon Sayings

- "I have counted the cracks in this cage so long that even silence has learned my name."
- "Do not look at me like I am the worst thing here. I still remember fear."
- "My master does not need chains. Chains are for creatures with hope left in them."
- "You call this prison. I call it the place where obedience outlived memory."
- "I was dangerous when they bound me. I am worse now, but not worse than the one I serve."
- "He does not rage. That would be mercy. He simply decides what a soul is worth, and the world agrees."
- "I could tell you how to leave, but every road in this place bends toward his notice."
- "The deadliest hand is not the claw at your throat. It is the one that taught the claw to wait."
- "I have dreamt of freedom for decades, and every dream ended with me begging to be caged again."
- "There are names I will not speak. Not because I am loyal. Because I have heard what happens to treachery."
- "Your kind fears monsters that lunge from the dark. Be wiser. Fear the will that taught the dark to hunger."
- "If he knew I had spoken this much, he would peel the memory from me and leave the pain."
- "I am a knife left too long in the wound. He is the hand that keeps twisting."
- "Go gently, little mortals. This place remembers defiance, and my master remembers everything."

### Dragon God

- Role: vast presence beyond reality
- Motivation: unknown
- Trait: communicates through pressure, recognition, scale, and impossible calm rather than ordinary speech
    - Dax note: this presence should feel less like meeting a stranger and more like standing before a truth his body
      remembers before his mind does
- Dax beats:
    - Let `Dax` feel the threshold before anyone else does, like a pressure in his chest or throat that answers
      something immense beyond the door.
    - Give him the sense that whatever is beyond the threshold already knows what he is, even if it does not yet know
      his name.
    - His body should react before his thoughts do: a change in breath, a tightening in the jaw, a lift of the head, a
      feeling in the blood, or a moment of stillness that reads as instinct rather than choice.
    - Show `Dax` one omen no one else gets. Good options:
        - a vast serpent-shape uncoiling and revealing wings made of weave magic
        - a broken throne beneath an eclipsed sky
        - a single enormous eye opening in the dark between stars
        - black scales lit from beneath like banked coals
        - a crown of horns turning slowly above a dead city
    - The emotional note should be recognition mixed with judgment, as though he is being noticed and weighed.
    - If you want a long-term hook, let `Dax` leave the threshold with the sense that this was not a summons, only the
      first time something ancient looked back.
- Optional whisper fragments for `Dax`:
    - "Blood remembers."
    - "Not yet."
    - "You were noticed long before you arrived."
    - "The sky has not opened."
    - "When the first wing rises, choose carefully."
    - "You carry an answer that is not yet yours."
    - "The old breath stirs beneath the world."

## GM Notes

- Keep the `Corterie` useful first and dangerous second. The betrayal lands harder if cooperation feels briefly real.
- Do not over-explain the wizard immediately. Let the party earn the truth through documents, the demon, and the shape
  of the space itself.
- This is a short session. Favor 3-4 strong beats over full map completion.
- If pacing runs fast, let the dragon-god threshold become the closing image.
- If pacing runs slow, the office and puzzle room alone can carry the full session while still delivering major clues.

### Running Notes

- For a 2 hour session, use one golem at a time unless the party openly escalates with repeated magic.
- If the betrayal becomes a full fight, start with `Tiny` and `George`, then bring in `Elys Merdye` as a flanker, thief,
  or extraction piece once positioning matters.
- If you want a lighter combat load, cut George's HP to 110 and the golem's HP to 125.
- The demon should feel dangerous, unstable, and useful, but it does not need to become the session's main fight unless
  the party makes that happen.
