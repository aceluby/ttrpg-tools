# 31 - Seven Hot Days To Everthrone

- Game: Corona Eclipsa
- System: Pathfinder 2e
- Duration: 2 hours
- Tone: serious fantasy, survival pressure, ominous homecoming

## Clarifying Answers Locked In

- Session length: 2 hours
- Core focus: 7 days of overland survival on the road back to `Everthrone`
- `The Corterie`: trail gone cold for now
- Time jump reveal: hold the full 4 month truth until the party reaches `Everthrone`
- The trapped wizard: no active role this session
- `Dax`: push a real destiny beat
- `Princess Serel`: appears after the journey, not during it
- Encounter mix: survival first, with random daily events and rare combat
- Horses: gone
- End state: the party reaches a badly changed `Everthrone` and `Nyx` rushes them inside

## Assumptions

- The party consists of 6 travelers for survival math: `Corona Eclipsa`, `Nighteyes`, and one additional ally,
  dependent, or travel burden under the group's care.
- The road home normally would be manageable, but without horses, good weather becomes punishing rather than helpful.
- This prep treats the travel stretch as a montage with hard daily choices instead of hex-by-hex play.
- The session should feel materially dangerous without becoming a bookkeeping slog.
- The king's death, `Dorian`'s disappearance, and the full time jump are all saved for the arrival beat.

## Characters

- `Harlan`
    - Spotlight: tracking, hunting, and reading signs that the others would miss
    - Useful pressure: the best hunter in the group still cannot conjure food from empty ground
- `Dax`
    - Spotlight: lead from the front when bodies are tired and tempers are short
    - Useful pressure: the dragon's "my child" should start to feel like a burden and a call
- `Jin`
    - Spotlight: ration discipline, morale, and choosing who eats when there is not enough
    - Useful pressure: hunger creates clean moral pressure without requiring villains
- `Marcel`
    - Spotlight: keeping hope alive, calming strangers, and making lucky breaks count
    - Useful pressure: charisma does not fill waterskins
- `Nighteyes`
    - Spotlight: nightly hunting and warning the group before danger fully arrives
    - Useful pressure: if he fails to feed himself, the group sees the wilderness wearing down even their most capable
      survivor

## Strong Start

- Recap:
    - Last we left off, Corona Eclipsa.... split up with the `Corterie` inside the anti-magical dungeon and searched the
      strange chambers around the great central pit. One group uncovered gold, `Boots of Bounding`, trapped doors, and
      an office full of secrets, while `Dax` and `Marcel` solved the symbol puzzle that caused a bridge to grow out
      toward the island in the center of the room.
    - The moment that bridge appeared, the `Corterie` moved. `Tiny` shouted to `Elys`, "Did you find it?" and when she
      answered "I did," both sides used `Dimension Door` at nearly the same instant. The dungeon answered with
      punishing lightning, the `Corterie` escaped ahead of you, and your pursuit dropped you into a vast abyss where
      `Dax` was called "my child" by a dragon so immense it felt like a god. When you were finally thrown back out
      through the artifact, you did not return to the cool spring you had left. You emerged into sweltering midsummer
      heat, evening closing in, your horses gone, and the trail already cold.
- Opening image:
    - Dusk is still hot and bright over a bare mountaintop normally covered by snow. The ground is baked, insects
      scream from the grass, and whatever prints the horses left are long gone.
- Immediate problem:
    - Before anyone can talk about pursuit, the road itself makes the choice for them: no mounts, limited supplies, no
      clear trail, and a full week on foot between them and `Everthrone`.

## Scenes

- Day 1: inventory, triage, and marching order.
    - Make them count what they actually have.
    - Set food, water, pace, and who is responsible for what.
    - `Dax` gets the first destiny beat here: when he looks south, he briefly feels the road like a line pulled taut
      inside his chest.
- Daily survival montage.
    - Each day asks the same questions in a new tone:
        - Do we have enough water?
        - Do we have enough food?
        - Do we push for distance or stop early to hunt and recover?
        - Who is hiding how tired they really are?
- Nightly hunt with `Nighteyes`.
    - `Nighteyes` must hunt each night.
    - Let his success or failure visibly affect the table mood.
    - Use his body language to telegraph danger before the random event hits.
- One day goes truly bad.
    - Heat, empty water, bad footing, scavengers, or a brutal encounter with something the summer dragged onto the road.
    - This is the day to show that survival is not an aesthetic; it costs something.
- One day goes surprisingly right.
    - A merchant, a shepherd, a hidden spring, edible berries, or a traveler willing to trade.
    - Good fortune should feel like a temporary reprieve, not a reset.
- Final approach to `Everthrone`.
    - The walls come into view.
    - Something is immediately wrong before the party knows the details.
    - The closing image should be `Red Reavers` inside the capital walls and `Nyx` finding the party fast, not because
      things are under control, but because they are not.

## Secrets and Clues

- The season is wrong, but the party does not yet know exactly how wrong.
- Their horses were taken long enough ago that pursuit is pointless.
- `The Corterie` is not the urgent problem right now; distance and survival are.
- Someone has been moving troops or dangerous outsiders along the road in recent weeks.
- Travelers are more frightened of the capital than relieved by its nearness.
- Roadside caches once maintained by the crown have been stripped, abandoned, or quietly repurposed.
- `Red Reaver` movement this deep inland means the kingdom's internal security has collapsed or been compromised.
- `Nyx` has been watching the roads for the party's return.
- `Dax` now feels dragon-linked omens in moments of direction, elevation, and heat shimmer.
- `Everthrone` is not merely grieving. It is unstable.

## Fantastic Locations

- The Summer-Burned Spring

    - Deeper details: broken reeds, insect clouds, and sun-whitened stones where cool moss should still cling.
- Dust Road Through Open Fields
    - Rendering: long miles, no shade, wavering air, and the slow realization that walking itself is now the enemy.
    - Deeper details: abandoned wheel ruts, old campsite rings, and crows that seem to arrive before trouble does.
- Thorn Brake Water Hollow
    - Rendering: a hidden dip in the earth, shade at last, muddy water, and the relief of not dying today.
    - Deeper details: animal tracks layered over boot prints, snapped branches, and signs that others needed this place

- Everthrone At Dusk
    - Rendering: familiar walls, wrong banners, armed silhouettes on the parapets, and strangers where trusted guards
      should be.
    - Deeper details: smoke from too many cookfires inside the city, the gates working under tension, and hard faces
      trying not to look afraid.

## Monsters or Threats

- Travel Survival Rules
    - Summer Death March rule:
        - this trip uses a custom attrition track called `Strain`
        - each traveler tracks their own `Strain`
        - `Strain` stacks across the full 7 day trip until removed by a full safe recovery opportunity
        - gain 1 Strain whenever a traveler:
            - ends the day unfed
            - ends the day with less than half required water
            - critically fails the daily water search lead check while pushing onward
            - is dropped to 0 HP during a road encounter
            - travels while already Fatigued
        - gain 2 Strain instead whenever a traveler:
            - goes both hungry and thirsty on the same day
            - critically fails a hunger or dehydration Fortitude save
            - marches at fast pace while already suffering from hunger or dehydration penalties
        - `Strain` effects:
            - 1 Strain: worn thin, -1 to overland Survival, Nature, and forced-march checks
            - 2 Strain: Fatigued if not already
            - 3 Strain: Clumsy 1 and -5 feet status penalty to land Speed
            - 4 Strain: doomed road state; at the next failed hunger, dehydration, or combat-related Fortitude save,
              the traveler collapses and begins dying or suffers a permanent travel scar if rescued immediately
            - 5 Strain: death is on the table; if the traveler is not removed from danger, given food and water, and
              allowed real rest, they die before reaching `Everthrone`
        - reducing `Strain`:
            - eating one normal meal removes no `Strain` by itself
            - a full day with enough food, enough water, and safe rest removes 1 Strain
            - strong shelter, magical healing paired with full provisions, or extraordinary aid can remove 2 Strain
              at GM discretion
        - long-term consequence option:
            - if you do not want death from attrition alone, a traveler who would die at 5 Strain can instead survive
              with a lasting consequence such as a ruined knee, sun-sick tremor, lingering cough, permanent scar, or a
              level-appropriate long recovery condition
    - Daily food requirement:
        - each human-sized traveler needs 1 ration per day
        - `Nighteyes` must hunt or be fed 1 ration-equivalent each night
        - party assumption for 6 travelers: 6 ration units per day, plus 1 for `Nighteyes` if he fails to hunt
    - Using packed rations:
        - if a traveler eats a full ration instead of hunted or improvised food, they gain +1 to that day's
          Fortitude saves against hunger, dehydration, and forced marching
        - if at least half the party eats packed rations on a given day, camp setup is cleaner and faster; grant +1
          to the first night check to recover, keep watch, or avoid a camp complication
        - packed rations do not require time to gather, which lets the party keep pace or spend daylight on water,
          scouting, or rest
    - Water requirement:
        - each traveler needs 2 waterskins per day in the summer heat unless the day is mostly shaded or cool
        - each day, the party must make an effort to find water unless they already have enough stored
        - one PC can lead a daily water search with Survival, Nature, or another smart approach you approve
        - Water Search DCs:
            - DC 19: dry open road, midday heat, or rushed search
            - DC 16: normal countryside, streambeds, or low ground with time to search
            - DC 14: near animal tracks, heavy insect activity, green growth, or remembered water landmarks
        - Success: find enough water for half the party
        - Critical success: find enough water for the full party for the day
        - Failure: find only 1d4 waterskins or muddy water requiring a choice to boil, risk, or move on
        - Critical failure: no water found and lose meaningful time
        - if the party finds only half water, everyone attempts the dehydration save below
    - Hunting:
        - one or more PCs can spend the final daylight stretch or early dawn making Survival checks to gather food
        - `Nighteyes` also hunts every night using his own instincts; roll once even if the party also hunts
        - Survival DCs:
            - DC 18: poor terrain, sparse game, or hurried attempt
            - DC 15: normal countryside with time to search
            - DC 13: near water, after signs of game, or with a strong lead
        - Success yields 1d4 ration units
        - Critical success yields 2d4 ration units
        - Failure yields no food
        - Critical failure spooks game and imposes -2 to the next hunt that day or night
    - `Nighteyes` hunt:
        - roll Survival +14 for `Nighteyes`
        - Success: he feeds himself
        - Critical success: he feeds himself and brings back enough for 1 extra ration unit
        - Failure: he comes back hungry and irritable
        - Critical failure: he returns hungry and injured or draws attention
    - Going hungry:
        - a traveler who gets no food for the day attempts a DC 17 Fortitude save at camp
        - Success: no immediate penalty
        - Failure: Fatigued the next day, +1 Strain, and +1 day added to the trip
        - Critical failure: Fatigued, Clumsy 1 until they eat a full meal and rest, and +2 Strain
        - second consecutive hungry day: increase the Fortitude DC by 2
    - Dehydration:
        - if a traveler gets less than half their needed water, attempt a DC 18 Fortitude save
        - Failure: Fatigued and +1 Strain
        - Critical failure: Fatigued, take 1d6 damage, reduce overland pace for that traveler, and +2 Strain
    - Pace choices:
        - fast pace: cover more ground, -2 to daytime perception and survival tasks
        - normal pace: default travel
        - slow pace: cover less ground, +2 to foraging, scouting, and noticing danger

- Daily Event Roll
    - Roll 1d10 once per day after morning decisions.
    - 10: lucky break. Merchant, shaded cart ride, clean water, or good trail. Gain 1 extra day of progress or 2d4
      supplies.
    - 9: hidden spring or fruitful hunting ground. Automatic water for the day and +2 to all hunting rolls.
    - 8: roadside traveler with useful news. Gain a clue and one small trade opportunity.
    - 7: clear weather and strong footing. Gain extra progress if the party travels normal or fast pace.
    - 6: no event. Just heat, distance, and attrition.
    - 5: uneasy signs. Crows, distant smoke, or spoor. +1 tension, but no direct payoff yet.
    - 4: spoiled cache or dried stream. Lose time and force a water search.
    - 3: bad ground. Twisted ankle, broken cart debris, thorn brake, or wasted daylight. Lose progress unless the
      party sacrifices supplies or rest.
    - 2: grim discovery. One or more of the missing horses are found dead and rotting, or a corpse warns the road is
      getting worse.
    - 1: severe encounter. Tough combat for a party of 6.

- Severe Encounter On A 1
    - Use 1 `elite owlbear` and 2 `wolf` scavengers if you want a raw wilderness fight.
    - Or use 4 level 5 road reavers and 1 level 7 reaver brute if you want a human threat tied to the worsening
      realm.
    - Reaver brute quick stats:
        - Perception +16
        - AC 25; Fort +17, Ref +12, Will +11; HP 130
        - Speed 25 feet
        - Melee `greataxe` +19, Damage 1d12+8 slashing
        - Brutal Swing [two-actions]: one greataxe Strike that deals +1d12 damage on a hit
        - Terrifying Roar [one-action]: 30 feet, DC 24 Will save or Frightened 1 (2 on a critical failure)

## Treasure / Rewards

- Any remaining supplies the party manages to preserve through the week
- Merchant trade goods, waterskins, dried meat, or a replacement bedroll from high-roll events
- A useful road rumor that makes the arrival scene land harder
- Narrative reward: the party earns their return to `Everthrone`; they do not simply fast-travel into the next crisis

## GM Notes

- Keep the travel week moving by treating each day as:
    - morning plan
    - event roll
    - one meaningful scene
    - night camp and hunt
- In a 2 hour session, you probably only need to play out 3 days in detail. Montage the others with quick
  consequences.
- Hunger should matter, but avoid cascading penalties so hard that the session stops being playable.
- The dragon beat for `Dax` should feel directional, not explanatory. He does not get answers yet; he gets a pull.
- Do not reveal the full 4 month jump until arrival.
- Final image:
    - the party reaches `Everthrone`
    - `Red Reavers` are visible inside the walls
    - before the party can even process it, `Nyx` appears and urgently takes them inside the castle
