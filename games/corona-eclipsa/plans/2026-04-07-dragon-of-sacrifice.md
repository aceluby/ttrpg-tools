# 33 - Dragon Of Sacrifice

- Game: Corona Eclipsa
- System: Pathfinder 2e
- Duration: 2.5 hours
- Tone: alarming prophecy, sacrificial resolve, escalating dread

## Clarifying Answers Locked In

- Session length: 2-3 hours
- Opening beat: `Dorian` speaking to the group through `Marcel`'s body
- Opening tone: alarming, unstable, and visibly upsetting to `Serel`
- `Marcel`'s weave-river choice should be both narratively and mechanically dangerous
- Failing or willingly surrendering to the weave should create real consequences and the beginning of weave addiction
- If `Marcel` fights his way back, he regains himself but keeps lingering effects
- The party should be told by `Marcel`'s body that the speaker is `Dorian`
- `Dorian` should sound convinced, noble, and increasingly beyond normal restraint
- `Serel` is visibly torn but still choosing to help him
- The mountain trek is a montage, not a full travel crawl
- `Dax` gets a strong dragon-linked beat at the mountain
- `Jin` gets a spiritual warning beat
- Marcel's night vision should show concrete future glimpses for every party member
- Those glimpses should be mixed in tone and may show futures that never happen
- The private second reach to `Dorian` stays secret between `Serel` and `Marcel`
- `Pycey` and `the Corterie` arrive in a sudden reveal
- The session should end on confrontation and explanation rather than immediate battle

## Assumptions

- This plan begins exactly where the last session ended: `Marcel` and `Dorian` touching inside the weave.
- The party has already rescued `Dorian`, reached shelter, and committed to helping him reach the mountain site.
- The dramatic core of the session is not travel difficulty; it is deciding whether `Dorian` is becoming the kingdom's
  salvation or its next disaster.
- Combat may begin at the end, but this session's main weight is scene pressure, revelation, and moral dread.
- If pacing runs fast, the final confrontation can slide naturally into the next session without losing momentum.

## Characters

- `Harlan`
    - Spotlight: reading the mountain, protecting the camp perimeter, and registering danger before others name it
    - Useful pressure: his instincts should tell him this place is active, not abandoned
- `Dax`
    - Spotlight: the dragon-carving site should feel personally important to his blood and destiny
    - Useful pressure: awe and fear should hit at the same time
- `Jin`
    - Spotlight: noticing the spiritual cost of what `Dorian` is becoming
    - Useful pressure: this may be sacrifice, but it does not feel clean
- `Marcel`
    - Spotlight: surviving the weave, seeing the future, and becoming the only one who can meaningfully awaken the rider
      statue
    - Useful pressure: the weave wants him to stop resisting
- `Serel`
    - Spotlight: grief, loyalty, and choosing a husband she may already be losing
    - Useful pressure: she knows "Sacrifice" is not a title anymore; it is a demand
- `Dorian`
    - Spotlight: tragic nobility turning into dangerous certainty
    - Useful pressure: he believes he is doing the right thing even as the weave hollows out pieces of him

## Strong Start

- Recap:
    - Last we left off, Corona Eclipsa.... met with `Nyx` and then with now-Queen `Serel`, who convinced you to take her
      into the mountains to find `Dorian`. With help from `Marcel` and `Dax`, the group narrowed down where the
      mountainside of king statues might be. You spent a few tense days in `Willow Woods`, bought supplies, disguised
      yourselves as monks, and slipped out to meet `Nyx` and fresh horses.
    - After days on the road and a hard climb into the mountains, you found `Dorian` under fire from Rhaenard's men,
      saved him, and rode
      hard for cover. Hidden inside a cave, `Serel` begged `Marcel` to reach through the weave and find her husband. You
      did. And when your hands touched in the weave, something opened.
- Opening image:
    - Firelight trembles against wet cave stone. `Marcel`'s body jerks upright with urgency in its bones,
      `Serel` is already half-risen in fear, and the air feels too thick, as though the weave itself has followed you
      into the cave.
- Immediate problem:
    - `Marcel` is no longer fully in his own body, and `Dorian` is speaking through him now.

## Scenes

- Action scene: Dorian in Marcel's body.
    - Read-aloud:
        - `Marcel`'s body lurches upright so fast it nearly knocks over the fire. His eyes are wrong, too focused and
          too far away at the same time. When he speaks, the voice is Marcel's, but the certainty inside it is not. "Do
          not be afraid. It is me. It is `Dorian`."
    - Hook:
        - The party has only a breath to decide whether they listen, restrain him, question him, or try to force
          `Marcel` back before this gets worse.
    - Scene role:
        - important information delivery
        - emotional impact
        - real potential for things to go wrong
    - Action stage-setter:
        - `Marcel`'s body sits up wrong, voice changed, movements not quite his, and tells the party that `Dorian` is
          speaking through him
    - Possible complications:
        - `Serel` panics or reaches for him at the wrong moment
        - `Marcel`'s body begins to seize as two minds pull at one frame
        - `Dorian` slips in and out, losing whole sentences to the weave
        - something in the cave answers when `Dorian` names the mountain and the dragons
    - Core content:
        - `Dorian` explains he has been searching for any answer to the `Red Reavers` and the `Hollowed`
        - he dug into the long-forgotten kings and their final mountain journeys
        - the weave showed him how they shaped the mountain and what they left behind
        - he must do the same and shape his dragon if the kingdom is to survive
- Action scene: Marcel in the weave river.
    - Read-aloud:
        - There is no cave anymore. Only a roaring river of silver-blue current dragging you past crowns, drowned faces,
          broken towers, and dragon shapes too large to understand. Every time you try to breathe, the weave rushes into
          you instead.
    - Hook:
        - `Marcel` must decide whether to surrender to the current and see where it carries him or fight toward himself
          before the weave decides who he is.
    - Scene role:
        - meaningful player decision
        - real potential for things to go wrong
        - emotional impact
    - Action stage-setter:
        - while `Dorian` speaks through his body, `Marcel` is swept into a roaring weave-current and must either
          surrender to it or fight back toward himself
    - Possible complications:
        - a future image tempts him to let go
        - he loses track of which memories are his
        - he feels the relief of surrender and the danger of wanting that relief again
        - the current drags him past shapes that look like dragons, crowns, and drowned faces
    - Mechanical suggestion: Weave Temptation track
        - `Marcel` gains 1 Weave Hunger whenever he willingly surrenders, critically fails a weave-facing check, or
          chooses power over selfhood
        - at 1 Weave Hunger, he is distracted by the weave's pull in quiet moments
        - at 2 Weave Hunger, he takes -1 to resist voluntary weave contact and feels drawn to stronger magical risks
        - at 3 Weave Hunger, the weave begins offering comfort, certainty, and prophecy in exchange for control
        - reducing `Weave Hunger` should require real rest, grounding, or refusing power when it would help
    - Resolution:
        - if he fights his way out, he regains his body and consciousness but carries a lingering aftertaste of the
          river
        - if he gives in, he comes back altered, more open to the weave, and more at risk of wanting it again
- Serel after the break.
    - Read-aloud:
        - `Serel` is on her knees by the fire without seeming to remember lowering herself there. Her hands are shaking,
          but her voice is steady only by force. "Tell me," she says. "Do not spare me. Tell me what is happening to
          him."
    - Hook:
        - The group has to decide how much truth to give `Serel`, what they believe about `Dorian` now, and whether they
          are still willing to follow him deeper into this.
    - Let `Serel` be shaken, relieved, and visibly trying to stay composed.
    - She is not blind to what this is doing to `Dorian`, but she still believes helping him may be the kingdom's only
      chance.
    - This is where the group can decide whether they are escorting a savior, enabling a martyr, or walking beside a
      disaster.
- Trek montage to the plateau.
    - Read-aloud:
        - The mountain narrows you into single file. Wind claws at cloaks and packs, gravel slips under every step, and
          ahead of you `Dorian` keeps moving with the hard certainty of a man following a voice only he can hear.
    - Hook:
        - This is the moment for the party to test `Dorian`, watch each other, and react to the mountain's pull before
          they reach whatever has been calling him.
    - Move this quickly but with strong images:
        - single-file along knife-thin paths
        - loose stone and hard wind
        - `Dorian` walking with strange purpose whenever the weave tugs him onward
        - `Serel` watching him when she thinks no one sees
    - Mid-montage character beats:
        - `Dax` feels the mountain answer something in his blood
        - `Jin` senses a sacred wrongness, like devotion stripped of humility
        - `Harlan` notices the route feels used by more than weather alone
- The statue plateau.
    - Read-aloud:
        - The path opens onto a high shelf of stone ringed with statues. Kings worn smooth by weather. Wolves staring
          outward. Bears with bowed heads. Dragons with half-lost faces. And towering over all of them, a dragon with a
          girl upon its back, banner raised into the mountain wind.
    - Hook:
        - The party can spread out, inspect the statues, question `Dorian`, and decide whether to help, study, or stop
          him before the mountain starts answering back.
    - The party emerges onto a high plateau ringed with dozens of statues:
        - kings
        - wolves
        - bears
        - old dragons
        - the largest of all, a dragon with a girl holding a banner
    - `Dorian` looks more alive than he has since the rescue.
    - He goes immediately to work, shaping the mountain with the weave.
    - Action scene structure:
        - stage-setter:
            - `Dorian` begins carving into the mountain face with force and precision that no exhausted man should have
              left
        - possible complications:
            - excess weave starts feeding the wrong statues
            - the mountain answers back with tremors or voices
            - `Dorian` overextends and bleeds or dissociates
            - observers realize one statue is drawing more power than the rest
    - Perception and insight:
        - the rider-girl statue beside the working site is taking in excess weave
        - on a strong enough read, someone may see the stone shift or breathe
- Night scene: Serel asks privately.
    - Read-aloud:
        - Long after the others settle, `Serel` finds `Marcel` at the edge of the camp where the firelight gives up on
          the dark. She does not meet his eyes at first. "I should not ask this of you," she says quietly. "I am asking
          anyway."
    - Hook:
        - `Marcel` must decide whether he will risk the weave again for `Serel`, and whether this choice comes from
          compassion, fear, curiosity, or something more dangerous.
    - In private, `Serel` asks `Marcel` to reach `Dorian` again.
    - She does not truly understand what she is asking, only that she cannot bear not trying.
    - Keep this between them unless one of them chooses otherwise.
- Action scene: Marcel's future-vision reach.
    - Read-aloud:
        - The instant you reach, the world stops being a cold mountain camp. Images hammer through you too fast to hold:
          claws on stone, chains breaking, banners burning, a dragon turning its head, cities under siege, and faces you
          love standing one choice away from becoming strangers.
    - Hook:
        - `Marcel` must choose how deep to go, which visions to hold onto, and whether he comes back carrying warning,
          temptation, or both.
    - Scene role:
        - important information delivery
        - emotional impact
        - meaningful player decision
    - Action stage-setter:
        - `Marcel` reaches into the weave again and is hit by fast, concrete glimpses of futures that may or may not
          come true
    - Possible complications:
        - one future feels so good he wants to chase it
        - one future is so terrible he nearly breaks contact the wrong way
        - the visions start speaking directly to his fear of irrelevance
        - he sees too much of `Dorian` and not enough of himself
    - Suggested future flashes:
        - `Harlan` is surrounded with `Nighteyes` lying lifeless by his side
        - `Dax` standing before his father, stabbing him through the heart while a woman watches
        - `Jin` before chains, fire, on a dock - destroying everything in his path
        - `Marcel` collapsing in front of his mother, rising to stand by her side
        - `Serel` in a prison cell, hungrily eating gruel with her hands as guard watch over her laughing
        - `Dorian` dead under a half built dragon statue
- Morning at the carving.
    - Read-aloud:
        - Dawn finds `Dorian` still at the cliff face, arms streaked with dust and blood. `Serel` stands nearby offering
          water with hollow-eyed patience and little sleep. The shape in the mountain can no longer be mistaken for
          anything else. It is a dragon, and it will be finished soon.
    - Hook:
        - The party must decide what they do before the carving is complete: assist, challenge him, investigate the
          statues, interfere, or prepare for what follows.
    - `Dorian` has worked through the night.
    - `Serel` is bringing him water, exhausted and devoted.
    - It is now obvious: he is carving a massive dragon, and at this pace he will finish in hours.
    - Let the group decide:
        - help him
        - question him
        - study the statues
        - try to interfere
        - prepare defenses
- Sudden reveal: `the Corterie` and `Pycey`.
    - Read-aloud:
        - A voice cuts across the wind before anyone sees its owner. "And there it is," it says, patient and
          disappointed. Shapes step out from behind statues, broken stone, and angles that should have been empty:
          `the Corterie`. And with them, `Pycey`, hands folded as if he has arrived to lecture a classroom instead of
          kill a king and a queen.
    - Hook:
        - The group has one beat to react before this becomes a standoff, and what they say or do first will decide
          whether the mountain hears argument, threat, or war.
    - They do not arrive with warning.
    - The party hears something, turns, and finds `the Corterie` and `Pycey` already there, positioned to kill `Dorian`
      and `Serel`.
    - This should begin with words, not immediate initiative.
    - `Pycey` and/or `George` should argue that `Rhaenard` is the better choice for the kingdom because:
        - power, not sentiment, preserves a realm
        - `Dorian`'s softness and visions make him dangerous
        - `Serel` ties the kingdom to weakness and foreign obligation
        - `Rhaenard` will do what a ruler must do
    - End on confrontation, threat, and the certainty that the mountain will not stay quiet.

## Secrets and Clues

- `Dorian` has been chasing forgotten royal practices tied to the kings' final mountain journeys.
- The weave has been guiding him directly, not merely through vague dreams.
- `Dorian` believes the answer to the `Red Reavers` and the `Hollowed` is the shaping of a dragon through royal weave.
- The dragon is not just a sculpture; it is intended as a weapon or vessel.
- `Marcel` is especially vulnerable to the weave's pull and may become addicted to it if he keeps yielding.
- The rider-banner statue is absorbing excess weave and may be awakenable by `Marcel`.
- `Dax`'s blood reacts to the mountain site as though it recognizes a true dragon-place.
- `Jin` can tell that whatever this ritual once was, it demanded more than strength; it demanded surrender.
- `Pycey` believes magical power is the real source of rightful rule.
- `The Corterie` are not here only for rivalry; they are here to stop `Dorian` and control what the mountain can
  produce.

## Fantastic Locations

- Cave Of The Shared Breath
    - Rendering: cold stone, weak firelight, sleeping gear hastily laid out, and one body moving with the wrong soul
      inside it.
    - Deeper details: the cave amplifies whispers, weave contact feels too easy here, and the walls sweat like something
      alive.
- Knife-Edge Ascent
    - Rendering: narrow paths, wind-scoured rock, single-file travel, and the feeling that one bad step drops you into
      white emptiness.
    - Deeper details: old chisel marks, half-buried waystones, and signs that this route once served ritual purpose
      rather than trade.
- Plateau Of Kings And Beasts
    - Rendering: dozens of statues, weathered rulers, wolves, bears, and dragons standing in a ring beneath a brutal
      sky.
    - Deeper details: every statue feels like a witness, and some look less eroded than they should.
- The Banner Rider
    - Rendering: a girl atop a dragon, banner raised, stone face turned into the wind as if expecting a command.
    - Deeper details: this statue drinks in stray weave, and with the right eye or magic it almost seems impatient.
- The Working Face
    - Rendering: a cliff wall half-raw, half-shaped, with `Dorian` cutting a dragon out of it as though the mountain
      were remembering itself.
    - Deeper details: the stone hums under weave pressure, fresh channels glow faintly, and the unfinished dragon
      already feels too large to be harmless.

## Monsters or Threats

- Weave Hunger Subsystem
    - Threat type: long-tail magical dependence
    - Trigger:
        - applies mainly to `Marcel` when reaching, surrendering, or repeatedly exposing himself to the weave river
    - Track:
        - 0: clear, though marked by what he has seen
        - 1: the weave feels comforting in a way it should not
        - 2: -1 to resist further voluntary weave contact or temptation
        - 3: the weave offers vivid futures, power, or emotional relief in exchange for surrender
    - Gaining `Weave Hunger`:
        - willingly going limp in the weave river
        - critically failing a weave-facing check
        - choosing prophecy or power over self-protection
    - Reducing `Weave Hunger`:
        - meaningful grounding, refusal, rest, or relationships pulling him back toward himself
- Action scene: weave river struggle
    - Stage-setter:
        - `Marcel` is swept in the weave current while `Dorian` borrows his body
    - Possible complications:
        - loss of memory
        - addiction pull
        - future shock
        - mistaken identity inside the river
- Action scene: statue plateau carving
    - Stage-setter:
        - `Dorian` begins shaping the mountain while the party decides whether to help, study, or intervene
    - Possible complications:
        - tremors
        - weave bleed into other statues
        - `Dorian` collapse
        - visible movement in the rider statue
- Action scene: sudden `Corterie` reveal
    - Stage-setter:
        - `Pycey` and `the Corterie` appear in position with killing intent and ideological certainty
    - Possible complications:
        - `Pycey` tries to split the party with arguments that are partly true
        - the unfinished dragon reacts to rising magic
        - `Serel` steps into danger rather than retreat
        - one `Corterie` member hesitates at the wrong moment
- If combat begins
    - Treat it as emerging from this scene, not as a disconnected encounter
    - The first round should be about positioning, the unfinished dragon, and whether anyone can stop `Dorian` or
      `Pycey` from forcing the mountain to answer

## GM Notes

- This session works best if the opening is immediately unsettling and never fully returns to safety.
- `Dorian` should not read as raving. He should read as noble enough to be persuasive and far enough gone to be
  frightening.
- `Serel` should stay sympathetic even when she asks too much of `Marcel`.
- The trek is a montage. Do not let it sprawl.
- Give `Dax` one beat of recognition and one beat of dread at the plateau.
- Give `Jin` one beat where his spiritual instincts tell him this is sacrifice emptied of balance.
- The future-vision scene should be concrete enough that players remember images, but open enough that you can
  reinterpret them later.
- If pacing runs short, end on the first sight of `Pycey` and `the Corterie`.
- If pacing runs long, let the ideological argument breathe before initiative.
