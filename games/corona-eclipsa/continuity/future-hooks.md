# Future Hooks

Last updated: 2026-03-16

This file captures reusable reveal material, pressure points, and scene hooks for future prep.

## Harlan

### Reveals

- The bond with `Nighteyes` is beast magic and socially taboo.
- `Nighteyes` is not just an animal companion but Harlan's deepest real connection.
- The `Piebalds` exist as a possible source of belonging, training, or temptation.

### Pressures

- Put valuable, portable objects in front of him when theft would clearly complicate the group's position.
- Force choices between secrecy and protecting the party.
- Repeatedly endanger `Nighteyes` to draw out Harlan's emotional truth.

### Scene Types

- A theft that seems irresistible but politically costly.
- An ambush where Harlan acts first to protect everyone.
- A meeting with a `Piebald` who recognizes the bond immediately.
- A quiet aftermath scene where `Nighteyes` becomes the bridge to human vulnerability.

### Good Questions To Revisit Later

- What would make Harlan trust a party member enough to confess the truth?
- Does he want a family, or only the idea of one?
- What does the Vaelport thieves' guild still mean to him?

## Dax

### Reveals

- Dragonbloods descend from dragon keepers.
- `Daxon Wraxor`'s father is `Bernard Wraxor`, a former `Everthrone` general living in exile.
- His mother `Matilda Wraxor` carried hidden dragon-linked lineage tied to `Mirage`.
- Dax is tied to a dragon-rider destiny.
- His family history is entangled with larger dragon-return events.

### Pressures

- Social rejection or exposure of his visible traits.
- Situations where negotiation is wiser but combat is more emotionally satisfying.
- Evidence about his father that is incomplete, contradictory, or costly to pursue.

### Scene Types

- A public moment where his scales or horns are seen.
- A clue trail about his father that suggests sacrifice instead of abandonment.
- A lead tied to `Matilda`'s pendant, old `Carvasean` history, or `Mirage`'s hidden legacy.
- A dragonblood or dragon-linked NPC who recognizes him for what he is.
- A battle where intimidation works but carries social fallout.

### Good Questions To Revisit Later

- What would Dax sacrifice to find his father?
- What does he believe happened to his mother?
- How does he react if dragon-rider destiny demands leadership rather than violence?

## Jin

### Reveals

- Jin is not merely devout; he has truly met `Soryan`.
- The calm exterior is covering unresolved rage.
- His anti-slavery conviction is absolute enough to reorder priorities.

### Pressures

- Encounters with slavery, trafficking, abuse, or abduction.
- Situations where patience would preserve the plan but delay justice.
- Moral choices where protecting strangers conflicts with strategic goals.

### Scene Types

- A captive begging for help during another urgent mission.
- A slaver or collaborator who assumes Jin will be pragmatic.
- A spiritual encounter that clarifies what `Soryan` expects of his champion.
- A moment where the party finally sees the anger under his discipline.

### Good Questions To Revisit Later

- What does Jin do when striking at slavery conflicts with the party's immediate goal?
- What line will Jin not cross even in service of liberation?
- How much of his past has he actually told the others?

## Marcel

### Reveals

- His prophetic dreams are tied to real forces, not just flavor.
- The party itself is the `Catalyst`.
- His "mother" is the campaign's larger manipulator or villain.
- His confidence rests on a fear that charisma is performance rather than truth.

### Pressures

- Social failure in public.
- Dreams that come true in visible, alarming ways.
- Discoveries about his origin that make his identity feel engineered.
- Attention from attractive, manipulative, or seemingly understanding women.

### Scene Types

- A prophetic dream that names an event just before it happens.
- A diplomatic scene where Marcel cannot talk his way to control.
- A confrontation with someone tied to his mother who treats him as an asset.
- A scene where he must tell the party a truth that changes their view of themselves.

### Good Questions To Revisit Later

- What does belonging actually mean to Marcel?
- If his charisma is a mask, who is he without it?
- How far would he go to protect the party from the truth about the `Catalyst`?

## Party-Level Hooks

- The group contains multiple hidden identities that can be revealed in staggered fashion.
- `Nighteyes`, dragonblood lineage, `Soryan`, prophetic dreams, and the `Catalyst` all support a campaign pattern of hidden truth becoming public consequence.
- Jin is the likely stabilizer when secrets break; Marcel is the likely speaker; Dax is the likely escalator; Harlan is the likely withdrawer unless `Nighteyes` is threatened.
- Future prep should favor scenes where character secrets collide with political consequences.

## Everthrone Court

### Reveals

- `Queen Rosamund Vaelor` is not merely protective of `Rhaegar`; she is helping build the court structure that could carry him to power.
- Some of `Dorian`'s apparent allies are really loyal to stability, not to him personally.
- One or more self-preservation houses may already be feeding information to both `Dorian` and `Rhaegar`.
- The court still contains people who would rally to `King Aldric`'s vision if they believed the center could hold.

### Pressures

- Force `Dorian` to choose between caution and visible leadership.
- Put public ceremonies, inheritance disputes, or household scandals in front of the party where neutrality helps `Rhaegar`.
- Let the manor won from `Lord Carston Rhyne` become politically useful, politically dangerous, or both.
- Make support costly by attaching it to marriages, patronage, public endorsements, or military obligations.

### Scene Types

- A feast, funeral, or court hearing where the wrong word shifts a whole house.
- A private meeting with a nervous minor noble who wants protection before naming names.
- A royal household scene where `Rosamund` is perfectly gracious while tightening the noose around `Dorian`.
- A summons to the party's manor that turns into an appeal, threat, or claim of ownership.

## Zephandor

### Reveals

- `Prince Kaelith Aiuvarin`'s death is still politically alive inside `Zephandor`, even after the marriage alliance.
- Some duty-bound houses back `Serel` publicly while quietly preparing for the alliance to fail.
- The vengeance houses do not all want the same outcome; some want justice, others simply want war.
- `King Therion Aiuvarin` may accept peace as policy while never forgiving `Everthrone` in his heart.

### Pressures

- Put `Serel` in situations where she must defend the alliance to people still grieving `Kaelith`.
- Let border raids or false-flag attacks reignite anti-`Everthrone` sentiment.
- Force `Dorian` and `Serel` to be symbols first and people second.
- Make every delay in the north look like weakness to Zephandor's hawks.

### Scene Types

- A tense royal council where `Serel` needs outside testimony to keep the alliance standing.
- A memorial observance for `Kaelith` that turns into accusation or reconciliation.
- A private conversation with a Zephandorian noble who offers help only if the party admits ugly truths.
- A border incident where the first report favors war and the second report changes everything.

## Vaelport

### Reveals

- The old `liveship` houses are not united; some want open trade, some want monopoly, and some are hiding disasters of their own.
- `Merovyx` is unstable because the ship carries two draconic inheritances in one hull.
- The `Tattooed` are not one movement but a coalition of liberationists, avengers, and people exhausted by survival.
- The new traders tied to `Thraxios` are trying to turn commerce itself into an argument for slavery.

### Pressures

- Put liberated captives, contraband, or witness testimony in the party's path when taking action would threaten the city balance.
- Make `Vaelessa`, `Oryndral`, or `Merovyx` demand something personal rather than transactional.
- Let merchants try to buy Jin's silence, Harlan's skills, or Marcel's charm.
- Force the party to decide whether ending the slave trade matters more than preserving Vaelport's fragile peace.

### Scene Types

- A dockside negotiation where a `liveship` speaks before its captain does.
- A midnight escape scene through hidden `Tattooed` safehouses.
- A merchant council where old blood and new money nearly come to violence.
- An unnerving audience with `Merovyx` that begins lucid and ends with a different voice answering.

## Vraegari

### Reveals

- `King Vargrim` is not driving the war freely; `Cerres` is steering him through the kidnapping of `Queen Brynja` and `Prince Eirik`.
- `Madrik Voss` is not merely advising caution; he is helping keep the kingdom trapped in the war.
- `Princess Astrid` may become the cleanest path to splitting the `Vraegari` away from the raids.
- The `Red Reavers` may continue raiding even if the royal host turns toward peace.

### Pressures

- Put evidence of the kidnapping within reach, but never in a form that is easy to prove publicly.
- Force warriors of the `Oathbound Raiders` to choose between oath, survival, and conscience.
- Let rescue opportunities for `Brynja` and `Eirik` compete with immediate civilian protection elsewhere.
- Make every northern victory or failure change which clans will listen to `Astrid`.

### Scene Types

- A parley with a clan leader who will only talk after a duel, oath, or feat of endurance.
- A raid aftermath scene where the party meets `Vraegari` survivors of the same war machine they are fighting.
- A covert operation to extract proof against `Madrik Voss`.
- A reunion or near-reunion scene where the fate of `Brynja` or `Eirik` changes the whole northern war.

## Cerres

### Reveals

- `Cerres` is not just backing chaos in one kingdom; it is shaping multiple realms toward mutual ruin.
- `Vaeloris Inkveil` is both a field operative and a personal bridge into Marcel's buried identity.
- The same hands influencing `Rhaegar`, `Madrik Voss`, and the wider war may be serving a single longer design.
- `Cerres` prefers captured leverage, engineered fear, and manipulated succession over open conquest.

### Pressures

- Put Marcel in scenes where `Vaeloris Inkveil` treats him like family, property, or prophecy.
- Let clues to `Cerres` come wrapped in useful truths so rejecting them carries a cost.
- Force the party to choose between stopping one immediate crisis and exposing the wider conspiracy.
- Use tattooed agents, prepared escape routes, and layered lies so victories against `Cerres` always feel partial.

### Scene Types

- A meeting with `Vaeloris Inkveil` that feels more like a family argument than an enemy briefing.
- An abduction, substitution, or illusion scene where the party realizes too late that `Cerres` was already present.
- A recovered ritual chamber that connects political sabotage to Marcel's origin thread.
- A confrontation where a `Cerres` agent offers the truth in exchange for the one thing the party cannot safely give.
