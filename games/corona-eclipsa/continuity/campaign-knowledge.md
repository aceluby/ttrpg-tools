# Corona Eclipsa Campaign Knowledge

Last updated: 2026-03-13

This file captures reusable campaign-level canon established in play.

## Campaign Premise

- Genre and scope: epic fantasy adventure
- Tone: grimdark political fantasy
- Table assumption: death is real and failure has lasting consequences
- Core inspiration:
  - direct lifts from `Realm of the Elderlings`: `Elderlings`, beast magic, and `Skill` adapted as `weave`
  - overall plot shape is also directly inspired by Hobb
  - broader style influence also comes from `Critical Role` actual plays

## Major Regions

- `Everthrone`
  - main kingdom
  - current party home base
- `Zephandor`
  - mountain kingdom
  - associated with half-elves
- `Vaelport`
  - southern trading city
- `Vraegari`
  - northern half-orc kingdom
- `Theaxios`
  - slave states of the south

## Active Political Situation

- `Vraegari` is attacking `Everthrone`.
- The attacks are being framed as `Zephandorian` responsibility.
- `Cerres` is actually pulling the strings behind this conflict.
- `Zephandor` sought to prove its innocence by securing a royal marriage alliance.

## Antagonists

- Active immediate antagonist:
  - `Prince Rhaegar`, second son of `Everthrone`
  - trying to become king
  - willing to work with larger destructive forces to get power
  - driven by narcissism rather than long-term stewardship
- Larger campaign antagonist:
  - `Cerres`
  - an island ruled by a secret cult of prophets
  - seeks to stop the `Catalyst`
  - believes dragon return would doom `Cerres`

## Dragon Status

- Dragons are still treated as myth by the wider world.

## Core Setting Truths

- `The weave` is the source of magic in the world.
- `Elderlings` are dragon-linked figures.
- Because dragons are gone, elderlings are commonly seen as mutilated beasts rather than sacred or noble beings.
- In `the Wild`, babies born with elderling-like malformations are regularly left to die.
- `Liveships` and `serpents` also exist in the setting as part of the campaign's elderling-derived world structure.
- `Serpents` are unformed dragons, effectively dragon caterpillars.
- `Liveships` follow the same core concept as in Robin Hobb's setting.

## Important Regions And Institutions

- `The Wild`
  - southern region
  - located north of `Vaelport` and south of the mountains
- court magic establishment
  - led by an unnamed head magician
  - the head magician secretly works for `Rhaegar`

## Major Campaign Events So Far

- Entire towns have turned `hollow`, with residents becoming almost zombie-like.
- The party has killed entire towns of hollowed people.
- At `Iron Hollow`, the party killed an entire hollowed town and became secretly known as the `Butchers of Iron Hollow`.
- On the journey to `Zephandor`, the party found a trapped dragon spirit and Dax gained an amulet that grants an ephemeral dragon mount.
- `Prince Rhaegar` tried and failed to frame the party for the death of the `Zephandor` prince.
- The group convinced `Prince Dorian` to marry the `Zephandor` princess.
- The party is now trapped in a pocket dimension with a rival adventuring group called `the Corterie`.

## Live Unresolved Threads

- The party needs a weapon capable of combating the `hollowed`.
- The party must escape the pocket dimension.
- The presence of `the Corterie` creates active rivalry pressure inside the pocket dimension.
- `The Corterie` is looking for a gem containing the essence of the sorcerer who created the pocket dimension.
- Releasing that essence would materially change the current crisis.
- Marcel has already dreamed that months will pass outside the pocket dimension, the king will die, `Dorian` will vanish, and `Rhaegar` will move for control.
- The likely "weapon" is the trapped wizard inside the dimension, but releasing him would be a grave mistake.

## Current Situation

- The party is currently trapped in a pocket dimension with `the Corterie` and needs to find a way out.

## Public Understanding

- The average person in `Everthrone` does not have a clear picture of the war, the hollowed, or royal intrigue.
- Rumor, fear, and uncertainty dominate public understanding.

## Hollowed

- The `hollowed` are the campaign equivalent of the `Forged` from `Realm of the Elderlings`.

## Everthrone Royal Situation

- `Everthrone` is ruled by a gravely ill king.
- The king is the father of both `Rhaegar` and `Dorian`.
- `Rhaegar` and `Dorian` have different mothers.
- The party's relationship with `Dorian` is that of trusted allies.
- The party has been working for the kingdom for most of the campaign.

## Zephandor Marriage Context

- `Prince Dorian` married the `Zephandor` princess.
- The marriage was politically important because `Zephandor` needed to prove it was not responsible for the attacks staged to implicate it.

## The Corterie

- `The Corterie` are magicians favored by the head magician.
- Marcel trained alongside them but was never accepted.

## Pocket Dimension Truth

- The pocket dimension was created by a powerful sorcerer.
- A gem inside it contains that sorcerer's trapped essence.
- `The Corterie` wants to find the gem and release him.
- Time is heavily distorted inside the dimension.
- The being trapped within has existed there for hundreds of years in real time.
- The trapped being is a powerful wizard who accidentally trapped himself there.
- If released, his goal is to live forever.
- He appears to be operating on his own agenda rather than serving `Cerres` or `Rhaegar`.

## Secret Knowledge Distribution

- `Rhaegar` knows `Cerres` is behind the wider conflict.
- The party does not know this yet.
- No other confirmed character has been established as knowing this truth.

## Prophecy Pressure

- `Cerres` has prophesied that stopping the `Catalyst` will stop the dragons.
- The `Catalyst` remains vague and may refer to a person, group, bloodline, event, or similar turning point.
- `Cerres` believes returning dragons would destroy them completely.
