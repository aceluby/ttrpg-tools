# Corona Eclipsa World Lore Dossier

Last updated: 2026-03-13

This file captures setting truths, public beliefs, hidden realities, and major lore structures.

## The Weave

- Core truth:
  - the `weave` is the source of magic in the world
- Access:
  - any magic user touches the `weave` to create magic
  - this may happen through inborn talent or through training
- Rarity:
  - magic use is still rare
- Public understanding:
  - most ordinary people barely think about the `weave`
  - its rarity keeps it distant from everyday life
- Danger:
  - the `weave` is addictive
  - without training or control, a user can go too deep
  - going too deep leaves the user comatose
- Open details:
  - whether some traditions use it differently than others

## Beast Magic

- Core truth:
  - beast magic is the power to bond with and speak with animals
- Rarity:
  - very rare
- Public reaction:
  - an exposed beast mage is treated like a leper who should be hanged for leprosy
- Social truth:
  - the hatred is driven mainly by misunderstanding and bigotry
- Real risk:
  - beast magic is not inherently evil
  - if the bonded creature dies, some people go mad
  - in those cases, the bonded creature continues through the host
  - some such presences are more forceful than others
- Law and enforcement:
  - beast magic is not formally illegal
  - in practice, communities do not ignore it and often impose mob justice
- Known culture:
  - the `Piebalds` are the main real beast-magic group in the world
  - they are not well known publicly
  - the group is split between one faction that wants to be left alone and another that is more militant
- Open details:
  - whether there are known traditions or teachers besides the `Piebalds`

## Elderlings

- Core truth:
  - elderlings are dragonkeepers and liaisons between dragons and the rest of the world
- Origin:
  - some are born that way
  - some are made that way
  - dragons help shape or confirm the process
- Public distortion:
  - with dragons gone, elderlings are widely seen as mutilated beasts rather than honored intermediaries
  - the old name `elderling` is no longer known to the public
  - people do not have a proper term for them and instead treat such births as mutation
  - those children are in fact dragon-touched
- Physical signs:
  - dragon features
  - dragons accentuate those features when choosing their keepers
- Open details:
  - how much the wider world remembers about their true role

## Dragons, Serpents, And Liveships

- Hidden truth:
  - dragons come from serpents
  - liveships are created from unborn dragons
- Public knowledge:
  - ordinary people do not know this
- Known keeper of this truth:
  - the trapped dragon in the `Vaelport` cave
- Open details:
  - how liveship creation is understood in-world

## Hollowed

- Core truth:
  - hollowed people are under a spell
  - they can only hold the most primal thoughts
- Cause:
  - `Cerres` monks can inflict this state
- Permanence:
  - the condition is not reversible
- Strategic purpose:
  - `Cerres` uses hollowing to spread discontent
  - this helps reduce the likelihood that dragons are released
- Secondary danger:
  - their flesh is especially useful to necromancers
  - necromancers can use it to summon extreme zombie-like horrors
- Open details:
  - who is spreading it

## Religion

- Broad state of the world:
  - the world is fairly godless
  - the gods have largely abandoned humanity
- Exception:
  - some gods still seem more active than others
- Open details:
  - which gods are meaningfully active
  - how ordinary people practice religion now
  - whether abandoned temples, old faiths, or local cults still matter
