# Faction Map: Good, Bad, and Ugly

Last updated: 2026-03-15

## Purpose

This document organizes the campaign's factions using a **good, bad, and ugly** lens inspired by the user's preferred worldbuilding method.

Definitions used here:

- **Good**
  - factions or people whose goals are broadly stabilizing, humane, or aligned with the party's better outcomes, even if they are flawed
- **Bad**
  - factions or people actively causing suffering, instability, manipulation, or predation
- **Ugly**
  - factions or people who are neither fully aligned nor fully villainous and could tip events in either direction depending on pressure, opportunity, or revelation

Important note:

- this is an interpretation framework, not objective cosmic truth
- categories can change as campaign revelations happen
- major factions can contain internal good, bad, and ugly sub-factions

## Major Faction Layer

This top-level layer reflects the current campaign framing the user wants to use as the starting map:

- **Good**: `Everthrone`
- **Bad**: `the Vraegari` / `the Red Reavers`
- **Ugly**: `Zephandor`, though the party has recently pushed them toward the **Good** side through alliance-building

Other major forces like `Cerres`, `Rhaegar's network`, `the hollowing force`, `the Corterie`, and dragon-linked powers still matter, but they can sit under this broader political frame for now until we break them out further.

### Good

#### Everthrone

Why they are **Good**:

- trying to preserve order in the capital and kingdom
- aligned with defense against raiders, internal destabilization, and hollowed threats
- currently closest to the party's active alliances
- this is the faction the party is currently most embedded in and working alongside

Key people:

- `Prince Dorian Vaelor`
- `Nyx`
- parts of the royal court not corrupted by `Rhaegar` or his allies
- `King Aldric Vaelor`

Risks:

- still embedded in monarchy and court politics
- can become compromised by secrecy, illness, succession panic, and wartime pressure

#### Zephandor, shifting toward Good

Why they are **Good**:

- sought to prove innocence rather than escalate conflict blindly
- marriage alliance is stabilizing compared to wider war pressure
- currently more likely to reduce bloodshed than intensify it
- the party has successfully moved them closer to the **Good** side through diplomacy and alliance-building

Key people:

- `Princess Serel of Zephandor`
- the `King of Zephandor`

Risks:

- may still prioritize state survival over truth
- alliance obligations can become coercive

### Bad

#### The Vraegari / Red Reavers

Why they are **Bad**:

- they are currently the kingdom's clearest military enemy
- the war is experienced on the ground as raids, violence, and direct threat
- at the highest level of public and current campaign framing, they occupy the enemy role

Why this may change later:

- their motives are more complicated than simple conquest
- future revelations may move some or all of this faction toward **Ugly** or even conditional alliance

Key pressure:

- they are the most visible external enemy facing `Everthrone`

### Ugly

#### Zephandor

Why they are **Ugly**:

- they were initially in a politically uncertain position
- they sat in the space between threat, accusation, and potential alliance
- they are driven by state survival as much as trust

Why they are shifting:

- the party has helped move `Zephandor` toward active alliance with `Everthrone`
- because of that, they now lean much closer to **Good** than **Ugly**

#### The Corterie

Why they are **Ugly**:

- arrogant, self-serving, and dangerous
- but not necessarily the deepest source of evil in the campaign
- they are opportunists, status-seekers, and useful traitors more than world-defining masterminds

Why they could tip either way:

- individual members may be turned, split, or exposed
- as a group, they are a threat, but still human-scaled and pressure-responsive

#### Liveships And Serpents

Why they are **Ugly**:

- these are not neatly moral categories
- they may become salvation, catastrophe, revelation, or all three
- dragon-linked world forces are likely to upset every political equilibrium

Why they could tip either way:

- their return or awakening may help heal the world
- it may also break kingdoms, cults, and prophecies in violent ways

Why **Good**:

- this means even the **Good** side of the assassins carries blood, secrecy, and unresolved guilt

Key people:

- `Nyx`

### Bad Within The King's Assassins

#### Silas Vey

Why **Bad**:

- rival assassin figure tied to `Nyx`
- now secretly works for `Rhaegar`
- may be trying to re-enter or infiltrate the assassin structure by leaning on his prior relationship with `Nyx`
- former field partner to `Nyx`
- a talented thief who was turned into a royal operative
- dangerous because he knows how she thinks and still has the charisma to make old trust feel plausible
- his break from `Nyx` came after she crossed a line for the kingdom that he could not accept
- he carries the memory of a mission where Nyx promised mercy, then secretly returned and killed a surviving witness
- his grievance is morally real, but he has let that wound curdle into service to a worse claimant
- this makes him dangerous not just as a killer, but as a man who can frame betrayal as righteousness

Likely role:

- returning ghost from Nyx's past
- internal traitor with emotional leverage
- living proof that even the "good" assassin line leaves wreckage behind

### Ugly Within The King's Assassins

#### Nico Vale

Why **Ugly**:

- information broker attached to the assassin world
- sells information to whoever pays
- not necessarily loyal to the kingdom, `Rhaegar`, or any single ideology
- secretly runs a network of child spies and informants

Likely role:

- swing figure who can feed truth, lies, or partial truths into any side of the conflict
- dangerous because he can be useful to everyone
- morally unstable because he can justify almost anything as "just information"

#### The Magicians

Why **Ugly**:

- magical institutions in Everthrone are powerful, exclusive, and politically important
- they are not automatically evil, but they are status-conscious, gatekeeping, and vulnerable to corruption
- parts of this faction may lean **Good**, **Bad**, or self-serving depending on who holds influence

Key people:

- `Wilhye Pycey`
- `the Corterie`
- Marcel's old training environment and its social hierarchy

## The Magicians

The magicians of Everthrone are not a single moral bloc. They are a prestige institution tied to access, influence, magical legitimacy, and court power.

At their best, they are protectors and trained servants of the kingdom.
At their worst, they are a ladder for influence, exclusion, and political manipulation.

### Good Within The Magicians

#### Loyal Servants Of The Crown

Why **Good**:

- they see magical talent as a responsibility rather than a license for superiority
- they are sincere about serving the kingdom and protecting people
- they represent the healthier version of what court magic could be

Key people:

- `Kristopher`
- `Marcel`

#### Kristopher

Why **Good**:

- a servant of the king identified as having magical ability
- genuine, sincere, and not driven by status hunger
- represents magical talent being used in service rather than domination

Important note:

- secretly the bastard son of `Dorian`

#### Marcel

Why **Good**:

- part of the magician world without being fully accepted by its inner hierarchy
- gives a view of the institution from someone excluded by its prestige logic
- has reasons to resist the corrupted version of court magic

### Bad Within The Magicians

#### Pycey's Power Bloc

Why **Bad**:

- believes power is what makes powerful leaders
- acts out of grudge, ambition, and desire to reclaim institutional influence
- sees magical structures as tools for rule and leverage rather than stewardship

Key people:

- `Wilhye Pycey`
- `Tiny Cote`

#### Wilhye Pycey

Why **Bad**:

- holds a grudge against the king and `Dorian`
- resents the king's decision to disband the old magical corterie in favor of a single mage
- sees the creation of a new corterie as his chance to reclaim influence and shape leadership through power

#### Tiny Cote

Why **Bad**:

- comes from relative poverty
- is motivated by the money he is being paid through `Pycey` by way of `Rhaegar`
- has tied himself to the corrupted power structure for personal advancement and survival

### Ugly Within The Magicians

#### The Corterie Followers And Drifters

Why **Ugly**:

- not everyone in this layer is ideologically committed
- some are there because they drifted into the hierarchy, wanted belonging, or failed to resist bad leadership
- they can still do harm, but they are not necessarily the source of it

Key people:

- `George Hammyth`
- `Elys Merdye`

#### Elys Merdye

Why **Ugly**:

- quiet and tends to go with the flow
- was added to the group in part because she did not push back against `Tiny Cote`
- dangerous through passivity, compliance, and lack of resistance rather than active malice

#### George Hammyth

Why **Ugly**:

- not especially ideological
- has linked himself to `Tiny Cote`
- likely more follower than architect
- can still become dangerous because he is easy to point in the wrong direction

## The Everthrone Court

The royal court of `Everthrone` is not one faction.
It is a pressure chamber built around illness, succession, access, and fear.

At present, it appears to break into three main blocs:

- loyalists to the king and `Dorian`
- the queen's faction around `Rhaegar`'s mother
- ambitious houses and courtiers who care first about preserving or increasing their own power

### Good Within The Everthrone Court

#### The King's Loyalists

Why **Good**:

- they support the existing legitimacy of the crown
- they remain loyal to the king's older standard of justice and duty
- they see `Dorian` as the best path to continuity, restraint, and stability
- they are motivated by stewardship more than appetite

Likely role:

- moral and political backbone of the court
- the people most likely to keep the realm from fracturing if given leadership to rally around
- natural allies to `Dorian`, `Nyx`, and anyone still serving the kingdom rather than themselves

Key people:

- `King Aldric Vaelor`
- `Prince Dorian Vaelor`
- unnamed old-guard loyalists, stewards, and sworn houses still anchored to the king's rule

Families:

##### House Valemere

Role:

- old-guard household of chamberlains, quartermasters, and civil stewards
- legitimacy-first family that sees continuity of rule as sacred

Family lens:

- mostly **Good**
- this is a **Good** family inside the **Good** bloc, with only minor internal cracks

Key people:

- `Lord Bennard Valemere` - **Good**
  - aging master of stores and grain contracts
  - painfully honest, stubborn, and loyal to the king's peace
- `Lady Talia Valemere` - **Good**
  - oversees relief, kitchens, and servants' networks
  - sees suffering in practical terms and resents court games
- `Lucan Valemere` - **Ugly**
  - younger heir frustrated by restraint and tempted by faster advancement
  - not cruel, but vulnerable to being lured by status

##### House Aerlyn

Role:

- knightly river-house tied to patrol roads, local levies, and the realm's practical defense
- backs `Dorian` because they believe he will hold the kingdom together

Family lens:

- mostly **Good**
- this family has a visible **Ugly** split caused by fear of civil war

Key people:

- `Lady Yselle Aerlyn` - **Good**
  - disciplined commander and court realist
  - supports `Dorian` out of duty rather than affection
- `Ser Roland Aerlyn` - **Good**
  - blunt household champion who despises intrigue
  - would rather die in open battle than bargain with `Rhaegar`
- `Tovin Aerlyn` - **Ugly**
  - younger son serving as liaison in court
  - sincerely wants peace and may drift toward compromise with the wrong people

### Bad Within The Everthrone Court

#### The Queen's Bloc

Why **Bad**:

- they are built around bloodline, favoritism, and proximity to the queen rather than the good of the realm
- they likely see the king's illness as an opening rather than a tragedy
- they provide social and political cover for `Rhaegar`'s rise
- even if not every member is openly monstrous, the faction's momentum points toward a more predatory court

Likely role:

- soft-power engine behind `Rhaegar`
- channel for patronage, influence, rumor, and succession pressure
- the group most likely to normalize cruel or destabilizing acts as necessary politics

Key people:

- `Queen Rosamund Vaelor`
- `Prince Rhaegar Vaelor`
- whichever court families have tied their fortunes to her household

Families:

##### House Vossaryn

Role:

- the queen's hard-core social and political enforcers
- intimate to her household and aggressively protective of `Rhaegar`'s claim

Family lens:

- firmly **Bad**
- this is a **Bad** family inside the **Bad** bloc, and they are largely aligned

Key people:

- `Lady Ysoria Vossaryn` - **Bad**
  - smiling architect of slander, invitations, and exclusions
  - treats court cruelty as refinement
- `Lord Orren Vossaryn` - **Bad**
  - keeper of favors, debts, and private leverage
  - believes fear is cleaner than loyalty
- `Seline Vossaryn` - **Bad**
  - young court beauty weaponized for rumor, seduction, and humiliation
  - enjoys breaking weaker people socially

##### House Marovant

Role:

- wealthy ceremonial house tied to prestige, marriages, and royal pageantry
- publicly elegant, privately committed to the queen's rise

Family lens:

- mostly **Bad**
- this family has a small **Ugly** crack, but the house as a whole still serves the queen

Key people:

- `Lord Hadrien Marovant` - **Bad**
  - vain and calculating patriarch
  - sees the succession as a chance to become indispensable
- `Lady Aurette Marovant` - **Bad**
  - devout partisan of bloodline politics

- `Julen Marovant` - **Ugly**
  - uneasy younger heir who recognizes `Rhaegar` is dangerous
  - still benefits too much from the queen's favor to break away cleanly

### Ugly Within The Everthrone Court

#### The Self-Preservation Faction

Why **Ugly**:

- they do not care much who rules so long as they remain close to the throne
- they can talk like patriots while making purely self-protective choices
- some will back `Dorian` if he looks stable, `Rhaegar` if he looks ascendant, or both if they can manage it
- they are not necessarily ideologues, but they make every crisis worse by treating it as an opportunity

Likely role:

- weather-vanes of the succession crisis
- brokers, fixers, marriage-makers, and quiet betrayers
- the bloc most likely to split, hedge, leak, or switch sides at the worst possible time

Families:

##### House Serrat

Family lens:

- fundamentally **Ugly**
- this family is openly split and can plausibly tilt in any direction

Key people:

Family lens:

- fundamentally **Ugly**
- this family is fractured enough that different members may end up on opposite sides of the conflict

Key people:

- `Lady Sabine Thorne` - **Ugly**
  - family strategist obsessed with survival above honor
  - encourages ambiguity as a deliberate policy
- `Maren Thorne` - **Good**
  - earnest court secretary quietly feeding warnings to safer hands
  - believes the family is rotting itself from the inside
- `Rhett Thorne` - **Bad**
  - ambitious cousin courting the queen's circle and `Rhaegar`'s allies

## Vaelport

`Vaelport` is less about crown legitimacy than about trade, memory, and what kind of wealth a city agrees to live on.
Its three major power centers fit the current campaign frame cleanly:

- old traders tied to `liveships`
- new traders enriched by `Thraxios` and the slave trade
- `the Tattooed`, former slaves who have carved out a hard-won refuge inside the city

### Good Within Vaelport

#### The Old Liveship Traders

Why **Good**:

- they are tied to older trade traditions, inherited obligations, and a more rooted understanding of the city's identity
- whatever secrets surround `liveships`, these traders are not primarily built on human commodification
- they tend to value continuity, reciprocity, and the long memory of port relationships
- they offer the healthiest version of mercantile power currently available in `Vaelport`

Likely role:

- old-money stabilizers of the port
- people who remember what the city was before faster, crueler wealth began to dominate it
- natural anchors for hidden history, maritime memory, and eventually `liveship` revelation

Internal note:

- they are still merchants
- they can be proud, secretive, and protective of their own
- their **Good** alignment comes from relative humanity and continuity, not sainthood

Houses:

##### House Marinel

Role:

- old trader house that believes a healthy port needs real competition, many captains, and shared prosperity
- sees `liveships` as a responsibility that should keep trade humane, not turn it into monopoly

House lens:

- **Good**
- this is the healthiest old-trader house in the city

Key people:

- `Lady Cerys Marinel` - **Good**
  - advocates for open trade lanes, fair tolls, and merchant rivalry without strangulation
- `Toman Marinel` - **Ugly**
  - fears the family will lose status if they stay too idealistic
- `The liveship Vaelessa` - **Good**

## Recommended Use

When building future lore, NPCs, arcs, or prep:

1. decide which major faction a new person belongs to
2. tag them as `good`, `bad`, or `ugly`
3. then ask whether their parent faction contains internal conflict
4. if yes, define their sub-faction role
5. if useful, define one named person who embodies each lens inside that faction
