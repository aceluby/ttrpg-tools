Daxon Wraxor
Human (Dragon-Blooded)
Bounty Hunter | Mid-20s
Descendant of the Arcane Dragon Exemplar: Mirage

Expanded Backstory
Daxon Wraxor was born on a storm-lashed night under a blood-red moon—an omen that, in the superstitious culture of Everthrone, spelled doom. His mother, Matilda, a woman of quiet grace and strange ancestral power, died giving birth to him, her lifeless hand still clutching the rune-etched pendant that would later be Daxon’s only clue to her mysterious lineage. What emerged from her womb was not the infant the midwives expected, but something… other. Something scaled, clawed, and ancient.
When the midwife screamed “Demonspawn!” and alerted the temple enforcers, Daxon’s father, General Bernard Wraxor, acted on instinct. He slaughtered the midwife, took his son, and disappeared into the wilderness, leaving behind rank, honor, and homeland.

Parents’ Story & Legacy
Bernard Wraxor, once a famed general of Everthrone, was not born noble. He earned every medal with blood and grit. Raised a commoner, Bernard clawed his way up from mud and rank to wear the eagle crest of a field general. It was during the siege of Carvasa—a brutal slave kingdom threatening Everthrone’s borders—that he met Matilda, a Carvasean slave who risked her life to warn the Everthronian army of an ambush.
Though history would later credit Bernard for the tactical victory, he always admitted in private that it was Matilda who truly won the war. He fell in love with her intelligence, her otherworldly beauty, and the sadness in her eyes. They returned to Everthrone as heroes, but few accepted a former slave as the wife of a general. She kept her lineage hidden, even from Bernard, never speaking of the strange marks on her skin or the dreams that haunted her.

Child of the Bloodline
Unknown to both Bernard and most of the world, Matilda was a direct descendant of Mirrage, the arcane dragon draconic exemplar—one of the primeval progenitors of dragonkind, born of pure magic and will. Mirage was said to have been a dragon of paradox and illusion, whose domains were mystery, hidden knowledge, and transcendent transformation. A rare and elusive figure even among dragons, Mirrage was less interested in conquest or treasure than in preserving ancient truths—truths often kept hidden by time or tyranny.
Matilda’s blood carried the latent arcane power of this ancient lineage, though it had gone dormant in her. In Daxon, it awoke.

Growing Up in Exile
The forest became Daxon’s cradle and crucible. Bernard, grieving and hardened, raised his son the only way he knew how—like a soldier. Daxon’s childhood was forged through training, survival, and harsh lessons. Yet even in this roughness, Bernard never let go of compassion. He spoke to Daxon of honor, of loyalty, and most of all, of his mother: “She was smarter than me, boy. Smarter than ten generals. And she saved me. More than once.”
Daxon learned to spar before he could write. His days were filled with mock battles using carved sticks and his nights with dreams—vivid dreams—of flight, of distant lands that no map ever bore, and dragons with eyes like his. These dreams became more detailed as he aged: flashes of ancient ruins overgrown with time, towers made of living crystal, and colossal dragons who knew him by name.
He didn’t understand them, not fully. But he knew, somehow, that they were not dreams of imagination—they were memories. Not his own, perhaps, but remembered through the blood.

Mutations & Isolation
Daxon’s golden, slit-pupiled eyes, scaled hand, and budding horns marked him as a freak among men. Even in his earliest interactions with children who wandered too far into the woods, he was treated with fascination that always curdled into fear. That betrayal stung. It was his first true wound—and it never fully healed.
As he aged, Daxon stopped trying to hide his mutations unless it was absolutely necessary. He found they offered him advantages in his work as a bounty hunter. Intimidation came easily when your eyes glowed like molten gold and your voice carried the subtle echo of something ancient and draconic.



The Bounty Hunter
Daxon took up bounty hunting to carve out his own place in the world. In the margins of society—among criminals, mercenaries, and sellswords—he found something resembling acceptance. He learned to track, hunt, and capture. His father’s teachings, refined by real-world practice, made him effective. More than once he brought in a bounty others deemed “impossible.”
He became known, albeit in whispers and rumors: The Golden-Eyed Reaper, The Dragonspawn, Wraxor’s Wraith.

The Call of the Blood
But the dreams persist. Night after night, they grow stronger. Daxon sees himself as not just a dragon rider—but as part of the dragon. His scaled skin expands in the dreams, melding with the hide of a magnificent beast. He doesn’t ride the dragon; he is the dragon— or becoming one. These visions haunt him, tempt him, beckon him.
Hearing stories from others like Marcel and Vivacia, he knows now that his dreams are not delusion. They are a summons. He doesn't just want to ride a dragon. He feels a truth burning deep inside: he was born to awaken one.

The Legacy of Mirrage
Somewhere in the world, hidden beyond the veil of common history, there are remnants of Mirrage’s ancient sanctum—likely cloaked in arcane illusion, guarded by riddles and spells that only dragonblooded descendants can see through. Daxon has no proof, only intuition and dreams. But he's begun to chase rumors, following whispers of arcane anomalies, ancient magic surges, and tales of dragons long thought extinct.
If he can find the truth of Mirrage’s legacy, he might finally understand what he is.
He may even become something more.




Current Motivations and Personality
Daxon walks the line between man and monster, justice and vengeance. He is deeply loyal to those who earn his trust, but slow to offer it. His rough, taciturn nature hides a curious mind and a kind heart—one that still aches to know what it means to belong.
He is suspicious of kingdoms, nobles, and religious zealots. The institutions that cast him out at birth can no longer claim to speak for him. But part of him—just a small part—still wonders what it would be like to live among them.
And deep down, behind the quiet demeanor and crimson axe, is a heart that burns like dragonfire.

Optional Adventure Hooks

The Slavers’ Return – Carvasa’s remnants have reformed under a new banner and seek to reclaim their old territory. Daxon must choose whether to stop them—or learn the truth of how deep their connection to dragons runs.


The Last Flight – A call goes out across the world: dragonkind is stirring again. Those of draconic blood are being drawn to a sacred convergence—where they will be tested to see who among them is worthy to become a true Dragon Rider.

Backstory for Campaign Integration
Overview:
Daxon Wraxor is a dragon-blooded human bounty hunter born of fire, flight, and war. His birth cost his mother her life, his father his honor, and himself any chance of a normal life. Now a scarred but capable warrior, Daxon stalks the fringes of society—tracking criminals, uncovering hidden truths, and searching for the draconic destiny that calls to him in his dreams.

The Wraxor Lineage
Born to General Bernard Wraxor, a renowned Everthronian war hero, and Matilda of Carvasa, a former slave with a mysterious ancestry, Daxon was cursed and blessed from birth. Matilda died during his delivery under strange arcane circumstances—her blood igniting Daxon’s dormant dragon lineage. The child that emerged bore golden slitted eyes, patches of shimmering scales, pointed teeth, and clawed hands.
In Everthrone, such deformities were seen as demonic, monstrous, or worse—signs of a cursed soul. When the midwives cried out and guards approached, Bernard chose exile over execution. He fled into the wilderness with his son, leaving behind a decorated career, a loyal battalion, and the ashes of a broken life.

Upbringing & the Mark of Mirrage
Daxon’s mutations stem from his connection to Mirrage, an arcane dragon draconic exemplar—a being of living magic, illusion, and hidden truths. The dragon bloodline manifests subtly in most descendants, but Daxon’s traits were unusually pronounced, suggesting he may be something far more than a mere scion—possibly a vessel for Mirrage’s legacy.
Bernard raised Daxon in the forested outskirts of the Everthronian kingdom. He trained his son as he would a young soldier: discipline, tactics, and martial prowess came first. Using what remained of his military knowledge, Bernard forged Daxon into a deadly combatant by his mid-teens. But more than weapons and war, Bernard tried to pass on Matilda’s spirit—her curiosity, wit, and moral compass.
As a result of Natural Ambition, Daxon demonstrated a rare intensity even in youth, mastering not just one but multiple forms of combat with ease. His talent outpaced Bernard’s expectations, and soon even his father struggled to challenge him.

Dreams of Flight
Since childhood, Daxon has been plagued—and at times, comforted—by vivid dreams. In them, he soars above unknown lands atop a gleaming arcane dragon. In these dreams, his body is more scaled, his hands taloned, and horns curve from his brow. The mount beneath him doesn’t carry him—it connects to him, as if it were an extension of himself.
These visions have grown more frequent and lucid in recent years. He knows now, thanks to encounters with others like Vivacia and Marcel, that these dreams aren’t mere fantasy—they’re echoes of something buried in his blood.
He believes that deep within the world—or perhaps beyond it—there is a place or a being calling him to fulfill a greater destiny: to awaken and bond with a true dragon, or possibly become something greater than either dragon or man.

Life as a Bounty Hunter
With Bernard aging and needing supplies, Daxon eventually took on work as a bounty hunter. His natural strength, honed instincts, and unsettling appearance made him especially effective. In urban environments, he keeps his scales and talons covered—though often only until intimidation is required. His combat style is brutal but precise; he prefers ambushes and shock tactics, wielding a greataxe with deadly efficiency.
Daxon’s Natural Ambition manifests in his ability to adapt to different styles of warfare and pursue multiple martial techniques simultaneously. This makes him unnervingly unpredictable in combat—something seasoned opponents often underestimate until it's too late.

Key NPCs & Relationships
Bernard Wraxor – His grizzled, disciplined father. Still alive (though aged), Bernard lives in the forest where he raised Daxon. His health is failing, and he worries what will become of his son when he’s gone.


Jorah Evansmith – Bernard’s old war buddy and Daxon’s godfather-figure. Brings supplies, information, and occasional jobs. A potential plot hook into Everthrone’s current politics or military activities.


Matilda Wraxor (deceased) – Daxon’s mother, whose pendant is etched with a draconic rune connected to Mirrage’s hidden sanctum.

Personality & Motivations
Daxon is quiet, introspective, and often blunt. While not cruel, he has little patience for hypocrisy or posturing. He’s not hostile to civilization, but he mistrusts it deeply—particularly nobility, religion, and institutions that once tried to kill him.
Despite his brooding nature, Daxon is deeply loyal to those few he allows close. He’s slow to trust, but once bonded, he is unwaveringly protective—sometimes to a fault.
He is drawn to fire, not just for its warmth but for the way it consumes and transforms—something that mirrors his own journey.

Campaign Integration Hooks
🐉 Blood of Mirrage
Strange arcane events—illusions made real, spells that act of their own will, or visions shared between dragon-blooded individuals—suggest that Mirrage’s lineage is stirring. Daxon’s pendant may be a key to an ancient vault or a draconic trial site hidden from mortal eyes.
🏰 Return to Everthrone
A crisis in the kingdom threatens to expose old secrets. Daxon’s name—long erased—resurfaces in whispers. Someone remembers Bernard. Someone else remembers the "demon child." Whether to restore his honor, uncover his mother's origins, or tear down the systems that banished him, Daxon is finally pulled toward the city he was exiled from.
🎯 The Wounded Star
A legendary bounty has been posted on a rogue mage claiming to be a dragon-rider. Daxon is hired to capture or kill them—but the more he uncovers, the more their visions align with his own. Could this fugitive be a blood cousin? Or a pawn of something darker?

🔥 The Fire Within
Daxon’s draconic nature is beginning to manifest further—perhaps a Draconic Transformation (rules from Secrets of Magic) is on the horizon. The more he uses his power, the closer he comes to losing his humanity… or discovering a greater one.
