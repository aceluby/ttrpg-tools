## Purpose

This file defines the assumptions the planner should use when generating session plans for the named game `corona-eclipsa`, which uses Pathfinder 2e.

- Pathfinder 2e is the rules system
- standard Pathfinder 2e creature guidance is the default source for enemies

- custom monsters, custom treasure, and large rules deviations should not be introduced unless explicitly requested

## Core Rules Guardrail

When generating output for this folder:

- use Pathfinder 2e terminology
- use Pathfinder 2e style stat blocks or base-game-equivalent creature references
- keep rewards and treasure close to PF2e baseline expectations unless the GM explicitly asks otherwise
- do not use D&D 5e mechanics, stat formatting, or reward assumptions
- default to standard Pathfinder 2e creature and treasure expectations

## Terminology

Prefer terms like:

- AC
- HP
- Perception
- saving throws
- DC
- class DC
- level
- actions and reactions
- initiative
- strikes
- spell DC
- hazard
- loot
- skill feats or class abilities when relevant

Avoid D&D 5e-specific terms unless the user explicitly asks for conversion.

## Threat And Stat Expectations

- combat-ready enemies should have PF2e-usable stat blocks or direct base-game references
- threats may include hazards, haunts, environmental obstacles, and social or faction pressure
- enemy math and presentation should stay recognizably PF2e
- if a full stat block is too large for the session plan, provide a clear base-creature reference plus only the most relevant adjustments

## Encounter Style

- default danger level: `dangerous`
- encounter tuning bias: `slightly harder than standard`
- the planner should assume the GM is comfortable with pressure and attrition
- the planner should support multiple smaller skirmish-style encounters before a major battle when the location and pacing support it
- when inside dungeons, the planner should assume a dungeon-turn structure with 10-minute stretches for exploration, investigation, movement, and pressure tracking

## Dungeon Procedure Assumptions

For dungeon-based sessions, assume:

- play may be structured around dungeon turns
- each turn represents roughly 10 minutes of in-fiction activity
- each stretch may give the party a chance for searching, investigation, travel, caution, or puzzle interaction
- dungeon initiative may be used to structure pressure and skirmishes before major combat
- session plans should support this by including time-aware scenes, pressure points, and small encounter opportunities

## Stealth And Surprise Assumptions

- the GM runs stealth with a custom wrinkle
- if the party successfully sets up a sneak approach, the sneaking side may get a first shot before sneak initiative is rolled with bonuses
- session plans should support ambush opportunities, infiltration beats, and stealth-forward openings when they fit the scenario

## Creature Guidance

- prefer standard Pathfinder 2e creatures first
- if an exact creature is not available, prefer a close base creature with light adaptation over fully custom design
- include creature level and the most table-relevant mechanics
- avoid inventing creature design that breaks expected PF2e encounter feel unless explicitly asked

## Reward Expectations

- rewards should feel plausible for PF2e baseline progression
- treasure should stay close to the game's expected economy and itemization unless the GM explicitly wants something unusual
- avoid importing D&D 5e-style loot assumptions
- treasure should default to standard Pathfinder 2e treasure guidance for level and pacing
- consumables, permanent items, coin, information, favors, and access should all remain plausible for PF2e play
- keep treasure close enough to standard expectations that it remains easy to track in tools such as Pathbuilder
